# Sources — demand-leadership

> *Primary sources for the [demand-leadership](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary Source 1:**
Christopher McKenna, *The World's Newest Profession: Management Consulting and the Creation of Corporate Culture* (2006, Cambridge University Press). ISBN: 978-0-521-81283-2.
URL: https://www.cambridge.org/9780521812832
> "We were not selling analysis. We were selling the confidence that comes from having a trusted outsider who knows your business."
> — Marvin Bower, quoted in McKenna, p. 58

McKenna's historical analysis documents the explicit sequence by which McKinsey moved from fulfilling an existing demand (efficiency review) to constructing a new category (top management consulting) and embedding itself as the cognitive default in the C-suite decision process.

**Primary Source 2:**
W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne, "Value Innovation: The Strategic Logic of High Growth," *Harvard Business Review*, January–February 1997. https://hbr.org/1997/01/value-innovation-the-strategic-logic-of-high-growth
> "Value innovators do not use competition as their benchmark... Instead, they make competition irrelevant by offering buyers a quantum leap in value... The question is not 'How do we beat the competition?' but 'How do we make the competition irrelevant?'"

This framing aligns with Stage 2 Value Reconstruction: the goal is not to compete within the customer's existing evaluation framework but to change the framework so that the evaluation criterion is one you define.

**What is NOT cited and why:** The three-stage sequence (Insight Generation / Value Reconstruction / Scene Creation) and the "cognitive default / second brain" framing are synthesized constructs developed for this skill. They integrate research on category creation (Breen 2011), demand-side sales theory (Moesta & Spiek), and embedded cognition literature — none of which maps directly to a single citable source.
