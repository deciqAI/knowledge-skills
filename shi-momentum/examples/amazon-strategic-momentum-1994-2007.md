# Method in Action: Amazon's Strategic Momentum, 1994–2007

> *Example for the [shi-momentum](../SKILL.md) skill.*

Amazon's growth from a garage startup to the dominant e-commerce platform is a textbook five-phase 势 case, documented in primary sources.

**Phase 1 — 蓄势 (1994–1995):** Jeff Bezos spent 1994–1995 building technical and operational capabilities before any public launch. He wrote the business plan in a car crossing the country, set up operations in a Bellevue garage, and personally packed boxes. No PR, no press, no amplification — pure capability accumulation. This is 蓄势: "经年累月的深耕与蛰伏."

**Phase 2 — 谋势 (1994–1995, parallel):** Bezos chose books as the entry category through systematic multi-angle reasoning: vast SKU variety (3 million titles), no dominant online player, lightweight logistics, and a customer base comfortable with mail order. He secured early relationships with book distributors. This is 谋势: "前瞻性的洞察" combined with "谋求合适的伙伴."

**Phase 3 — 借势 (1995–2000):** Amazon launched in July 1995 — directly onto the Internet S-curve. The 1995–2000 period saw Internet adoption grow from ~16 million to ~361 million users globally. Amazon did not create this wave; it rode it with maximum precision. This is 借势: "宏观环境长期向上" and "指数级增长行业."

**Phase 4 — 得势 (1997–2002):** Bezos's 1997 shareholder letter is explicit about focus discipline: "We will continue to make investment decisions in light of long-term market leadership considerations rather than short-term profitability considerations." Amazon did NOT expand to all categories at once — "少就是多." It compounded on books, then music, then video, building logistics infrastructure at each step before the next expansion. This is 得势 discipline.

**Phase 5 — 造势 (1995 onward, compounding after 得势):** Customer reviews (launched 1995) created resonance — users generated content that attracted more users. The 1-Click patent (1999) became a negotiating tool and a PR signal of technological leadership. These are 造势 mechanisms: "落地的真实事件" and "引发共振的效应."

**Primary source:** Bezos, J. (1997). *Letter to Shareholders*, Amazon Annual Report. Filed with SEC: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1018724/0000950130-97-001298.txt — "We will continue to make investment decisions in light of long-term market leadership considerations rather than short-term profitability considerations or short-term Wall Street reactions."
