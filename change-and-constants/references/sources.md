# Sources — change-and-constants

> *Primary sources for the [change-and-constants](../SKILL.md) skill.*

1. **Heraclitus of Ephesus** (c. 500 BC), Fragment DK B30. Translated in Kirk, G.S., Raven, J.E., & Schofield, M. (1983). *The Presocratic Philosophers: A Critical History with a Selection of Texts* (2nd ed.). Cambridge University Press. https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/heraclitus/. Verbatim: "This world-order, the same for all, no one of gods or men has made; but it always was and is and shall be an ever-living fire, kindling in measures and going out in measures." This fragment establishes the philosophical foundation: change (the ever-living fire) and constancy (the logos, the same for all) are simultaneously real — not opposites, but co-dependent realities. The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry provides the scholarly apparatus for interpreting this fragment in context.

2. **Collins, J. & Porras, J.** (1994). *Built to Last: Successful Habits of Visionary Companies*. Harper Business. https://www.harpercollins.com/products/built-to-last-jim-collinsjerry-i-porras. Verbatim (Chapter 3): "Preserve the core / Stimulate progress — this is the fundamental duality that allows a company to be both principled AND adaptable." And: "Visionary companies make some of their best moves by experimenting, trial and error, opportunism, and — quite literally — accident. What looks in retrospect like brilliant foresight and pre-planned strategy was often the result of 'Let's just try a lot of stuff and keep what works.'" Collins and Porras studied 18 visionary companies vs. 18 comparison companies over 50+ years. The consistent differentiator was not strategy or charisma but the explicit maintenance of the core-preserve / progress-stimulate duality.

3. **Madison, J., Hamilton, A., Jay, J.** (1788). *The Federalist Papers*, No. 49. Avalon Project, Yale Law School. https://avalon.law.yale.edu/18th_century/fed49.asp. Verbatim: "The reason assigned in the Federalist for the provision... is that it is a great desideratum in government to give it all the efficacy requisite to the attainment of the ends of its institution." Madison's argument in Federalist No. 49 provides the primary-source reasoning for why some elements must be made structurally resistant to change — not because change is bad, but because certain foundational elements require stability to function.

**What is NOT cited and why:** The aphorism "智者预变而变，愚者见变不变" (The wise adapt before change arrives; the foolish see change and refuse to adapt) is a widely circulated Chinese proverb whose precise classical origin is contested — multiple attributions exist and no single primary-source document can be verified. It is referenced as a cultural anchor, not as a citable source.
