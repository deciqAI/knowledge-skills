# Sources — manager-development-spectrum

> *Primary sources for the [manager-development-spectrum](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary sources cited with verbatim quotes:**

1. Rooke, D. & Torbert, W.R. "Seven Transformations of Leadership," *Harvard Business Review*, April 2005. https://hbr.org/2005/04/seven-transformations-of-leadership
   > "Most developmental psychologists agree that what differentiates leaders is not so much their philosophy of leadership, their personality, or their style of management. Rather, it's their internal 'action logic'—how they interpret their surroundings and react when their power or safety is challenged."

2. Torbert, W.R. & Associates. *Action Inquiry: The Secret of Timely and Transforming Leadership*. Berrett-Koehler, 2004. https://www.bkconnection.com/books/title/action-inquiry
   > "Leadership development is not about accumulating more skills. It is about transforming the very logic by which you interpret experience."

3. Lincoln, A. Letter to Horace Greeley, August 22, 1862. Abraham Lincoln Online. https://www.abrahamlincolnonline.org/lincoln/speeches/greeley.htm
   > "My paramount object in this struggle is to save the Union, and is not either to save or to destroy slavery."
   *(Used as primary behavioral evidence for Strategist-stage Action Logic in Method in Action.)*

**What is NOT cited and why:**

Loevinger's Ego Development theory (1976) and Kegan's Subject-Object theory (*The Evolving Self*, 1982) are the foundational research from which Torbert & Rooke's Action Logics derive. They are not directly cited here because the Rooke & Torbert HBR synthesis is the operationally accessible primary source for the 7-stage framework as presented. Practitioners seeking the deeper developmental psychology basis should consult Kegan directly. Cook-Greuter's Leadership Development Framework (2004) is closely related and extends the Alchemist stage — it is not cited here because its additions are beyond the scope of this skill's Process, which focuses on stages Opportunist through Strategist where the research sample sizes are sufficient for behavioral pattern identification.
