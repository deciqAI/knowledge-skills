# Sources — economic-moat

> *Primary sources for the [economic-moat](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Buffett, W. E. (1986, 1995, 1999, 2007). *Berkshire Hathaway Letters to Shareholders.* https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html. The original "moat" metaphor and the five-source operationalization.
- Buffett, W. E. (1999). "Mr. Buffett on the Stock Market." *Fortune Magazine*, November 22, 1999. The five moat sources articulated.
- Greenwald, B. C. N. (2001). *Competition Demystified.* Portfolio. ISBN 978-1591841807. The structural-test treatment.
- Dorsey, P. (2008). *The Little Book That Builds Wealth.* Wiley. ISBN 978-0470226513. The Morningstar moat methodology.
- Mauboussin, M. J. & Callahan, D. (2013). *Measuring the Moat.* Credit Suisse / Counterpoint Global research paper. The quantitative analytical framework.
- Porter, M. E. (1980). *Competitive Strategy.* Free Press. ISBN 978-0684841489. The five-forces predecessor (industry analysis).
- Thiel, P. (2014). *Zero to One.* Crown. ISBN 978-0804139298. The startup application of moat thinking.
- Collins, J. (2001). *Good to Great.* HarperBusiness. ISBN 978-0066620992. Empirical study of durable competitive advantage.
- Lapidus, S. (1998). "The Moat Concept." Morningstar internal research. The original Morningstar formalization.
- Christensen, C. M. (1997). *The Innovator's Dilemma.* Harvard Business School Press. ISBN 978-0875845852. The disruption mechanism behind moat erosion.
- The Economist (2012). "The last Kodak moment?" January 14, 2012. https://www.economist.com/leaders/2012/01/14/the-last-kodak-moment. The Kodak film-moat erosion case.
