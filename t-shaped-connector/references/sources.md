# Sources — t-shaped-connector

> *Primary sources for the [t-shaped-connector](../SKILL.md) skill.*

## Sources

**Primary:**
1. Shannon, C.E. "A Mathematical Theory of Communication," *Bell System Technical Journal*, Vol. 27, pp. 379–423, 623–656 (July and October 1948). https://people.math.harvard.edu/~ctm/home/text/others/shannon/entropy/entropy.pdf
   Verbatim quote used: "The fundamental problem of communication is that of reproducing at one point either exactly or approximately a message selected at another point."

2. Pierce, J.R. "The Early Days of Information Theory," *IEEE Transactions on Information Theory*, Vol. 19, No. 1 (January 1973), pp. 3–8. https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/1054812
   Verbatim quote used: "Shannon's genius was not just mathematical. He had the ability to see that the same mathematical structure appeared in many places — in telephone systems, in genetics, in language — and that this was not coincidence but deep structure."

**Supporting:**
- Gardner, H. *Creating Minds: An Anatomy of Creativity Seen Through the Lives of Freud, Einstein, Picasso, Stravinsky, Eliot, Graham, and Gandhi*. Basic Books, 1993. Reference to Shannon's master's thesis characterization used for context only; no direct quote cited.

**What is NOT cited and why:** The "T-shaped" metaphor itself has been attributed variously to McKinsey, IDEO, and Tim Brown without a clear primary source. This skill uses the metaphor as established vocabulary without citing its origin, since the origin is disputed and the skill's contribution is the three-dimension extension (Pareto-connector position) and the connector test, not the base T-shape concept.
