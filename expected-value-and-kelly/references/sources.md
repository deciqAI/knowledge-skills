# Sources — expected-value-and-kelly

> *Primary sources for the [expected-value-and-kelly](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **Kelly, J. L., Jr.** "A New Interpretation of Information Rate." *Bell System Technical Journal*, Vol. 35, No. 4 (July 1956), pp. 917–926. **The primary source for the Kelly formula and its proof.** https://www.princeton.edu/~wbialek/rome/refs/kelly_56.pdf
- **Thorp, Edward O.** *Beat the Dealer: A Winning Strategy for the Game of Twenty-One*. Random House, 1962 (2nd ed. Vintage, 1966). **Primary source** for the first practical application of Kelly to a positive-edge real-world bet (blackjack card-counting).
- **Thorp, Edward O.** "The Kelly Criterion in Blackjack, Sports Betting, and the Stock Market." Paper for the 10th International Conference on Gambling and Risk Taking, Montreal, 1997. The clearest concise summary by Thorp himself; covers the fractional-Kelly trade-off. https://www.eecs.harvard.edu/cs286r/courses/fall12/papers/Thorpe_KellyCriterion2007.pdf
- **Thorp, Edward O.** *A Man for All Markets: From Las Vegas to Wall Street, How I Beat the Dealer and the Market*. Random House, 2017. Autobiography; primary source on the Princeton-Newport years and Thorp's evolution from blackjack to markets.
- **Bernoulli, Daniel.** "Exposition of a New Theory on the Measurement of Risk." Commentarii Academiae Scientiarum Imperialis Petropolitanae, Tomus V (St. Petersburg, 1738). English translation by Louise Sommer in *Econometrica*, Vol. 22, No. 1 (January 1954), pp. 23–36. **Primary source** for the utility-versus-EV distinction.
- **Benter, William.** "Computer Based Horse Race Handicapping and Wagering Systems: A Report." In *Efficiency of Racetrack Betting Markets*, edited by Donald B. Hausch, Victor S. Y. Lo, and William T. Ziemba, pp. 183–198. Academic Press, 1994 (reprinted World Scientific, 2008). **Primary source** for combining a fitted probability model with public odds and sizing pari-mutuel bets by fractional Kelly under estimated inputs and market impact.
- **Poundstone, William.** *Fortune's Formula: The Untold Story of the Scientific Betting System That Beat the Casinos and Wall Street*. Hill & Wang, 2005. The popular history; useful as a secondary reference and for the Bell Labs / Shannon / Kelly intellectual lineage.
- **U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission filings and quarterly earnings materials of Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Nvidia (2023–2025).** Durable **primary sources** for AI-driven data-center capital-expenditure guidance and Nvidia's data-center-segment revenue growth during the 2023–2026 AI investment boom — used in preference to fast-moving press figures, which should be verified against these filings. https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar
- **Contemporaneous business-press coverage of frontier-AI-lab financing rounds and valuations (e.g., The Wall Street Journal, Financial Times, The Information, 2023–2026).** **Secondary** reference for reported startup valuations (OpenAI, Anthropic, and others); treat specific figures as approximate and time-stamped, since boom-era valuations are reported inconsistently and change rapidly.
- **NVIDIA Corporation, Annual Report on Form 10-K and quarterly Form 10-Q / earnings materials (fiscal years 2024–2025).** **Primary source** for the growth of the Data Center reporting segment during the 2023–2026 AI compute boom; used in preference to press summaries of "Nvidia's AI revenue," which should be verified against the segment disclosures in these filings. https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar
- **MacLean, Leonard C., Edward O. Thorp, and William T. Ziemba (eds.).** *The Kelly Capital Growth Investment Criterion: Theory and Practice.* World Scientific, 2011. **Authoritative collected reference** on Kelly and fractional-Kelly investing, including the growth-vs-drawdown trade-off that justifies defaulting to a fraction of full Kelly when the edge is estimated rather than measured — directly relevant to sizing correlated, power-law AI bets.
- The popular framing "the house always wins" is **not** cited here as a source — by this skill's own rule, an aphorism is not evidence. The Kelly criterion is precisely the formula for *the conditions under which the house does not always win* — when the player has a measurable positive edge.
