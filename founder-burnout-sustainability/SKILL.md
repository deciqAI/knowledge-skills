# Founder Sustainability — Protect the Company's Key Asset

## Overview
In a small company the founder *is* the critical infrastructure: when their energy, health, or judgment fails, the company fails. Burnout isn't a badge — it degrades exactly the decision quality the business depends on, and it's usually the result of an unsustainable system, not weak willpower. Treating founder sustainability as an operational requirement (not self-indulgence) protects the highest-leverage asset you have.

## The Process
1. **Read the early signals** — dread, cynicism, dropping output/decision quality, sleep/health slipping. *Gate: if judgment is already degrading, this is an operational risk, not a mood — act now.*
2. **Find the real driver** — chronic overload, no recovery, isolation, or lack of progress/control (each has a different fix).
3. **Protect the non-negotiables** — sleep, exercise, relationships; these are inputs to decision quality, not rewards for finishing.
4. **Cut or delegate the load** — ruthless prioritization, SOP + delegate/automate the draining repetitive work (see sop-systemization, when-to-make-first-hires).
5. **Build recovery into the system** — real breaks, boundaries, peer support; a pace you can hold for years, not weeks.
6. **Re-check periodically** — sustainability is a maintained state, not a one-time fix. *Gate: "I'll rest after this milestone" repeated indefinitely = the trap.*

## When to Use
- Sustained overload / declining energy or judgment
- Before a long push, to design a survivable pace
- Founder isolation or loss of motivation

## Applying It Well
- Frame recovery as protecting decision quality — it reframes rest as strategy.
- Delegating/automating the draining tasks fixes the system, not just the symptom.
- Peer/founder community counters isolation, a top burnout driver.

## Red Flags
- Treating exhaustion as a badge of commitment.
- "Rest after the next milestone" on infinite repeat.
- Judgment/health degrading with no change to the system.

## Verification
- [ ] Early warning signals monitored honestly
- [ ] Root driver identified (overload/recovery/isolation/control)
- [ ] Health non-negotiables protected as inputs
- [ ] Draining load delegated/automated; sustainable pace set
