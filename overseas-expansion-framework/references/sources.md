# Sources — overseas-expansion-framework

> *Primary sources for the [overseas-expansion-framework](../SKILL.md) skill.*

## Sources

**Primary:**
1. Nintendo corporate history (official). https://www.nintendo.co.jp/corporate/en/history/
   Documents the 1985 NES US launch strategy, the Nintendo of America entity structure, and the test-market-first entry sequence.

2. Sheff, D. *Game Over: How Nintendo Zapped an American Industry, Captured Your Dollars, and Enslaved Your Children*. Random House, 1993. ISBN: 0-679-40469-4.
   Verbatim quote used (p. 47): "Nintendo's strategy was not to overwhelm American retailers with a Japanese assault — it was to let the product speak first, in test markets, where the product itself would carry the proof of concept before the company committed resources to full distribution."

**Supporting:**
- FTC market analyses of the US video game market collapse (1983–1985) are referenced for the "Atari Shock" market context. Specific FTC filing numbers vary by analysis year; the 97% revenue decline figure is widely cited across secondary sources including Sheff (1993) and is treated as established historical fact rather than a citable primary source in this skill.

**What is NOT cited and why:** The Spirit/Physical binary (精神出海 / 实体出海) is a framework originally developed in Chinese business strategy commentary for describing Chinese companies' overseas expansion, and is used here as an organizing vocabulary rather than a proprietary framework. No single originating source for the vocabulary exists; it emerged from practitioner discourse. The 12-item Pre-Expansion Checklist is an original synthesis developed for this skill and is not claimed as a previously published list.
