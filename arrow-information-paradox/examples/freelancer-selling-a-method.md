# Method in Action: The Freelance Expert Who Must "Show It Works" (SMB / small-business case)

> *Example for the [arrow-information-paradox](../SKILL.md) skill.*

An independent consultant or freelancer sells expertise — a method, a system, a diagnosis. A prospective client says: *"Show me it works before I pay."* This is Arrow's paradox at the smallest commercial scale, and it is where solo operators most often give away the thing they sell. This composite case (a freelance operations/SEO/marketing consultant, but the pattern is identical for many trades) walks the decision through The Process.

**Step 1 — Audit appropriability.** The information is the consultant's method — the specific analysis, playbook, or fix that produces the result. The concrete leak: if the consultant *does the actual work* in a free "show me" session — delivers the audit, hands over the plan, fixes the problem — the client now has the deliverable and has no reason to pay. The value was in the doing, and it has been done for free. A clear, nameable leak. Gate passed.

**Step 2 — Identify the minimum the client needs.** The client does not need the *whole method* to believe the consultant is worth paying. They need evidence that *this person can produce the outcome*: a diagnosis of what's wrong (not the full fix), a sample of prior results, a small verifiable win. The convince-vs-reproduce gap is the freelancer's entire livelihood — closing it wrong means working for free.

**Step 3 — Choose the mechanism.** With no leverage to demand NDAs and no patent to file, the fitting stack is **proxy demonstration + reputation**, delivered through a **paid pilot**.
- *Proxy demonstration:* prove value on a *bounded* proxy — a redacted case study of prior results, a diagnostic that names the problem without delivering the solution, a small fixed-scope task that shows the outcome without transferring the whole method.
- *Reputation:* testimonials, references, and a track record substitute prior evidence for present free labor.
- *Paid pilot:* crucially, charge for the proxy. A small paid diagnostic or pilot both proves value *and* filters serious buyers — and the client's willingness to pay for the pilot is itself their value estimate.
This closes the leak: the client observes outcomes and capability without receiving the reproducible method for free.

**Step 4 — Build the disclosure ladder.**
```
Rung 1 (free, no commitment):   redacted prior results, a general read on their situation, references
Rung 2 (paid pilot, small fee): a bounded diagnostic — name the problem, show a sample win; NOT the full fix
Rung 3 (signed engagement):     the full method, plan, and implementation
```
The reproducible core — the full method and implementation — sits on Rung 3, released only against a signed, paid engagement. The free rung shows *that it can work*; it never *does the work*.

**Step 5 — Pre-commit the walk-away.** Before the sales conversation, the freelancer decides: *the free session diagnoses and demonstrates; it does not deliver.* The pressure move to resist is "just fix this one thing for me and then I'll hire you" — which, if the one thing *is* the deliverable, is the paradox extracting the whole sale for free.

**Disclosure Plan:**
```
# Disclosure Plan: consulting method → prospective client
## The information: the method/playbook that produces the client's desired outcome
## Appropriability leak: doing the actual work in a free "show me" hands over the deliverable, unpaid
## Minimum-to-convince: evidence this person can produce the outcome — a diagnosis + sample results
## Reproducible core (Rung 3 only): the full method, plan, and implementation
## Mechanism: proxy demo (redacted results + bounded diagnostic) + reputation; charge via a paid pilot
## Disclosure ladder:
   Rung 1 (free):          redacted case studies, a general read, references
   Rung 2 (paid pilot):    bounded diagnostic — name the problem, show a sample win, not the full fix
   Rung 3 (signed deal):   full method and implementation
## Walk-away: the free session diagnoses and demonstrates; it never delivers the fix
```

**What it explains.** The freelancer who "shows it works" by *doing the work* has sold their service for nothing — a direct hit from Arrow's paradox. The fix is to demonstrate *outcomes* on a bounded proxy (ideally a paid one) while keeping the reproducible method behind a signed engagement. Charging for the pilot does double duty: it proves value the way the paradox allows *and* it surfaces the client's real willingness to pay.

The mapped steps:
1. Audit: doing the work free = giving the deliverable away → real leak
2. Minimum: client needs proof-of-capability, not the whole method → the freelancer's core gap
3. Mechanism: proxy demo + reputation, monetized as a paid pilot
4. Ladder: redacted results (free) → bounded diagnostic (paid pilot) → full method (signed)
5. Walk-away: demonstrate, don't deliver, in the free session

**Founder takeaway:** If a prospect asks you to "show it works before I pay" and you run a solo or small-business practice, do not prove yourself by *doing the paid work for free* — that is selling your product for nothing. Prove value on a bounded proxy: redacted past results, a diagnosis that names the problem without solving it, or a small *paid* pilot. Charge for the pilot; a client's willingness to pay for the proxy is the clearest read you will get on whether they'll pay for the whole thing.
