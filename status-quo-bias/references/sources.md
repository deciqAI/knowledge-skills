# Sources — status-quo-bias

> *Primary sources for the [status-quo-bias](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Samuelson, W., & Zeckhauser, R. (1988). "Status Quo Bias in Decision Making." *Journal of Risk and Uncertainty*, 1(1), 7–59. The founding paper; coined the term; inheritance experiment.
- Johnson, E. J., & Goldstein, D. (2003). "Do Defaults Save Lives?" *Science*, 302(5649), 1338–1339. European organ donation natural experiment; most-cited demonstration of default effects.
- Thaler, R. H., & Sunstein, C. R. (2008). *Nudge.* Yale University Press. ISBN 978-0300122237. Full framework for libertarian paternalism: designing defaults that serve user interests.
- Madrian, B. C., & Shea, D. F. (2001). "The Power of Suggestion: Inertia in 401(k) Participation." *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 116(4), 1149–1187. 401(k) auto-enrollment; participation doubles from default change alone.
- Johnson, E. J., Hershey, J., Meszaros, J., & Kunreuther, H. (1993). "Framing, Probability Distortions, and Insurance Decisions." *Journal of Risk and Uncertainty*, 7(1), 35–51. NJ–PA auto insurance tort-default natural experiment; founding field evidence that defaults are policy.
- Kahneman, D., Knetsch, J. L., & Thaler, R. H. (1991). "Anomalies: The Endowment Effect, Loss Aversion, and Status Quo Bias." *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, 5(1), 193–206. The unifying KKT paper; mechanistic explanation.
- Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* Farrar, Straus and Giroux. ISBN 978-0374533557. Chapter 28 covers defaults and status quo bias for a general audience.
- Sunstein, C. R. (2013). *Simpler: The Future of Government.* Simon & Schuster. ISBN 978-1476726595. Applied policy implementation of default design at scale.
- McKinsey & Company. "The state of AI" global survey series (2023–2025). Documents rapid rise in reported generative-AI adoption alongside a persistent lag in scaled deployment and measured enterprise-level impact — contemporary evidence of inertia in AI-adoption decisions. https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai
- MIT Sloan Management Review & Boston Consulting Group. "AI and Business Strategy" annual research reports (2023–2024). On the recurring gap between AI investment/experimentation and realized value, and the organizational inertia behind it. https://sloanreview.mit.edu/big-ideas/artificial-intelligence-business-strategy/
