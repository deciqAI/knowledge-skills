# Sources — strategy-execution-levers

> *Primary sources for the [strategy-execution-levers](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary sources with verbatim quotes:**

1. Kaplan, R.S. & Norton, D.P. "The Office of Strategy Management." *Harvard Business Review*, October 2005. https://hbr.org/2005/10/the-office-of-strategy-management
   > "Research shows that 60% to 80% of companies fall short of the success predicted from their new strategy. The cause in most cases is not a bad strategy but bad execution."

2. Nutt, P. "Surprising but True: Half the Decisions in Organizations Fail." *Academy of Management Executive*, 1999, 13(4). https://doi.org/10.5465/ame.1999.2570553
   > "Decision failure rates hover around 50 percent... Failure arises when decision makers... skip steps in the [implementation] process."

3. Murray, C. & Cox, C. *Apollo: The Race to the Moon* (Simon & Schuster, 1989). NASA Mission Reports (public domain): https://www.nasa.gov/mission_pages/apollo/missions/
   (Historical case source for Method in Action section)

**What is NOT cited and why:**

The four-lever taxonomy (Cognitive Alignment, Behavior Guidance, Motivation Activation, Evolution Acceleration) and the specific effectiveness figures (+40%, -65%, +32%, 3x) are derived from Chinese organizational strategy execution literature and practitioner frameworks, not a single attributable academic publication. The effectiveness estimates are directional and drawn from reported implementation outcomes in enterprise settings; they are not controlled experimental results. Users requiring empirical validation of specific lever effectiveness should consult: Kotter, J.P. *Leading Change* (Harvard Business School Press, 1996) for a complementary 8-phase change execution model; and Beer, M. & Nohria, N. "Cracking the Code of Change" (*Harvard Business Review*, May 2001) for Theory E vs. Theory O execution distinctions.
