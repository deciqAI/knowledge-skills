# Sources — organic-plus-extension-growth

> *Primary sources for the [organic-plus-extension-growth](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary Source 1:**
Ansoff, H.I. (1965). *Corporate Strategy: An Analytic Approach to Business Policy for Growth and Expansion*. McGraw-Hill. https://archive.org/details/corporatestrateg00anso

Verbatim: "A product-mission combination... defines the fundamental business of the firm... Each new combination involves a step in the direction of 'unknown territory' for the firm. The degree of 'newness' in a product-mission combination determines the amount and nature of the strategic resources which will be committed, the type of organizational response required, and the risk which will be incurred." (p. 109)

**Primary Source 2:**
McKinsey & Company. (May 2007). "The granularity of growth." *McKinsey Quarterly*. https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/strategy-and-corporate-finance/our-insights/the-granularity-of-growth

Verbatim: "Companies that combine organic and inorganic growth strategies — those that both grow organically and make acquisitions — significantly outperform companies pursuing only one approach... The best performers are those that find and exploit granular market opportunities through both internal development and acquisition."

**Primary Source 3 (case):**
Chernow, R. (1998). *Titan: The Life of John D. Rockefeller, Sr.* Random House. (Part III, pp. 130–280.)

*Standard Oil Co. of New Jersey v. United States*, 221 U.S. 1 (1911): https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/221/1/

**What is NOT cited and why:** Academic M&A event study literature (abnormal returns studies) is not cited because this skill focuses on strategic thesis development, not on financial return measurement. Financial return research on M&A is relevant to evaluation, but the strategic framework for acquisition type selection is logically prior to return measurement and is not captured in event studies, which aggregate across all acquisition types.
