# Sources — resource-integration-hierarchy

> *Primary sources for the [resource-integration-hierarchy](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary Source 1:**
Ostrom, E. (1990). *Governing the Commons: The Evolution of Institutions for Collective Action*. Cambridge University Press. https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511807763

Verbatim: "What we observe in the world is neither the tragedy of the commons nor the panacea of privatization. What we observe is a wide variety of institutional arrangements that individuals have created to govern common-pool resources... individuals have organized themselves to solve collective action problems that conventional theory predicted they could not solve." (p. 1)

**Primary Source 2:**
Hamel, G., & Prahalad, C.K. (1994). *Competing for the Future*. Harvard Business School Press. https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=7827

Verbatim: "The goal is not to find a good position within the existing industry structure but to create new competitive space... Companies must learn to leverage resources: to get the most out of the resources they have, to borrow resources from others, and to use resources more efficiently." (p. 160)

**Primary Source 3 (case):**
Nightingale, F. (1858). *Notes on Matters Affecting the Health, Efficiency, and Hospital Administration of the British Army*. Harrison and Sons. Available: https://archive.org/details/notesaffecting00nigh

Mortality data and coxcomb diagram source: Cohen, I.B. (1984). Florence Nightingale. *Scientific American*, 250(3), 128–137. https://doi.org/10.1038/scientificamerican0384-128

McDonald, L. (ed.) (2001). *Florence Nightingale: An Introduction to Her Life and Family*. Wilfrid Laurier University Press.

**What is NOT cited and why:** Management consulting frameworks on resource allocation optimization (activity-based costing, zero-based budgeting) are not cited because this skill addresses the prior question of mindset about what resources exist and are accessible — the optimization of allocated resources is a Level 2–3 problem addressed by those frameworks. This skill is about the posture that determines whether resource creation and integration are even attempted.
