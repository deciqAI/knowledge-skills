# Sources — 10-10-10

> *Primary sources for the [10-10-10](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Welch, S. (2009). *10-10-10: A Life-Transforming Idea.* Scribner. ISBN 978-1416591818. The canonical articulation.
- Bezos, J. (1998 / 2020). Academy of Achievement interview, republished in *Invent and Wander: The Collected Writings of Jeff Bezos.* Harvard Business Review Press. ISBN 978-1647820718. The regret-minimization framework.
- Frederick, S., Loewenstein, G., & O'Donoghue, T. (2002). "Time Discounting and Time Preference: A Critical Review." *Journal of Economic Literature*, 40(2), 351-401. The empirical economics of hyperbolic discounting.
- Laibson, D. (1997). "Golden Eggs and Hyperbolic Discounting." *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 112(2), 443-478. The foundational hyperbolic-discounting paper.
- Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* Farrar, Straus and Giroux. ISBN 978-0374275631. The System 1 / System 2 framework that underlies 10-10-10's structural logic.
- Marcus Aurelius. *Meditations* (c. 170-180 CE). The classical Stoic source for prospective-hindsight reasoning. Multiple translations; Gregory Hays (Modern Library, 2002), ISBN 978-0812968255, is the modern standard.
- Suzuki, S. (1970). *Zen Mind, Beginner's Mind.* Weatherhill. ISBN 978-1590302675. Contains discussion of present-moment awareness as a complement (not opposite) to temporal-distance practice.
