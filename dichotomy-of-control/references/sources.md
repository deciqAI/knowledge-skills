# Sources — dichotomy-of-control

> *Primary sources for the [dichotomy-of-control](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Epictetus. *Enchiridion* (Handbook), §1, c. 125 CE. The foundational text. Standard translation: Long, G. (1888); modern: Hard, R. (2014, Oxford World's Classics, ISBN 978-0199595181).
- Epictetus. *Discourses*, c. 108 CE. Comprehensive collection of lectures. Standard translation: Hard, R. (2014).
- Marcus Aurelius. *Meditations*, c. 170-180 CE. Standard modern translation: Hays, G. (2002, Modern Library, ISBN 978-0679642602).
- Beck, A. T. (1976). *Cognitive Therapy and the Emotional Disorders.* International Universities Press. The CBT founding text, citing Epictetus.
- Stockdale, J. B. (1993). "Courage Under Fire: Testing Epictetus's Doctrines in a Laboratory of Human Behavior." Hoover Institution Essay.
- Hadot, P. (1995). *Philosophy as a Way of Life.* Blackwell. ISBN 978-0631180333. The "philosophy as practice" framework.
- Long, A. A. (2002). *Epictetus: A Stoic and Socratic Guide to Life.* Oxford University Press. ISBN 978-0199245567. The standard scholarly treatment.
- Robertson, D. (2010). *The Philosophy of Cognitive-Behavioural Therapy.* Karnac. ISBN 978-1855757561. The Stoicism-CBT mapping.
- Pigliucci, M. (2017). *How to Be a Stoic.* Basic Books. ISBN 978-0465097958.
- Holiday, R., & Hanselman, S. (2016). *The Daily Stoic.* Portfolio. ISBN 978-0735211735.
- Niebuhr, R. (1934). The Serenity Prayer (composed for a sermon; later adopted by Alcoholics Anonymous).
- Rotter, J. B. (1966). "Generalized expectancies for internal versus external control of reinforcement." *Psychological Monographs*, 80(1), 1-28. The internal-locus-of-control framework.
