# Sources — founder-trajectory-matrix

> *Primary sources for the [founder-trajectory-matrix](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary:**
1. Carnegie, A. *Autobiography of Andrew Carnegie*. Doubleday, Page & Co., 1920. Project Gutenberg: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/17976
   Verbatim quote used: "I had to learn that I couldn't do everything myself. The men around me were not my subordinates — they were my partners in progress." (p. 184)

2. Carnegie, A. "The Gospel of Wealth," *North American Review*, Vol. 148, No. 391 (June 1889), pp. 653–664. JSTOR: https://www.jstor.org/stable/25101612
   Verbatim quote used: "The man who dies thus rich dies disgraced. … It is a question of how wealth will be administered for the community." This essay marks Carnegie's late-trajectory articulation of distributed stewardship over Solo Executor control — the output of his documented trajectory shift.

**Supporting:**
- Livesay, H.C. *Andrew Carnegie and the Rise of Big Business*. Longman, 1975. Used for corroboration of Carnegie's partner-selection decisions in the 1870s–1880s. No direct quotes cited — used as contextual confirmation only.
