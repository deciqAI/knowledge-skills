# Sources — lateral-thinking

> *Primary sources for the [lateral-thinking](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **de Bono, Edward.** *Lateral Thinking: Creativity Step by Step.* Harper & Row, 1970. **Primary source defining lateral thinking and its core techniques.** The verbatim definition in the Overview is from the Introduction. https://archive.org/details/lateralthinking00debo
- **de Bono, Edward.** *The Use of Lateral Thinking.* Jonathan Cape, 1967. **Original foundational text; first formal presentation of the concept.** Verbatim quote in Overview from ch. 1.
- **Kelley, Tom.** *The Art of Innovation.* Doubleday, 2001. **Primary source for the IDEO shopping-cart case.** Ch. 1 provides a full account of the five-day process.
- **ABC News *Nightline*.** "The Deep Dive." Broadcast July 13, 1999. **Primary broadcast documentation of the IDEO case.** Available in archived form at various university media libraries.
- **Beaty, Roger E., et al.** "Creativity and the Default Network: A Functional Connectivity Analysis of the Creative Mind at Rest." *NeuroImage* 64 (2013): 92–98. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2012.09.019 — Neural basis for lateral/cross-domain connectivity in creative thought.
- **Oxford English Dictionary.** Entry: "lateral thinking." The OED formally credited de Bono with coining the term; the entry documents its first recorded use in 1967.

**Not cited and why:** The phrase "think outside the box" is a popular culture shorthand that has no primary-source connection to de Bono's method and carries none of its operational content — it is not used as evidence here. Attributed quotes circulating online as de Bono's ("You cannot dig a hole in a different place by digging the same hole deeper") are plausible paraphrases of his themes but lack verifiable primary-source attribution and are not used as evidence.
