# Sources — cognitive-evolution-stages

> *Primary sources for the [cognitive-evolution-stages](../SKILL.md) skill.*

1. Bloom, B.S., Engelhart, M.D., Furst, E.J., Hill, W.H., & Krathwohl, D.R. (1956). *Taxonomy of Educational Objectives: The Classification of Educational Goals, Handbook I: Cognitive Domain.* David McKay Company. https://www.uky.edu/~rsand1/china2012/texts/Bloom%20et%20al%201956%20Taxonomy.pdf
   > "The taxonomy is a framework for classifying statements of what we expect or intend students to learn as a result of instruction... The six major classes are Knowledge, Comprehension, Application, Analysis, Synthesis, Evaluation."

2. Piaget, J. (1963). *The Psychology of Intelligence* (original 1950, English trans. Malcolm Piercy & D.E. Berlyne). Routledge. https://archive.org/details/psychologyofinte00piag
   > "Intelligence is an adaptation... To say that intelligence is a particular instance of biological adaptation is thus to suppose that it is essentially an organization and that its function is to structure the universe just as the organism structures its immediate environment."

3. Curie, M. (1903). *Radioactive Substances* (doctoral thesis). Translated and reprinted by Chemical Heritage Foundation. https://www.biodiversitylibrary.org/item/107855
   > "I proposed to examine whether other substances besides uranium compounds were capable of emitting similar Becquerel rays. The results of the examination of the various compounds showed that the property of emitting these rays is a specific property of the uranium atom."

**What is NOT cited and why:** The specific five-stage, eleven-sub-level structure (5阶段11层级) presented here is a synthesis framework derived from Bloom, Piaget, and observed professional development patterns, not a direct transcription of any single primary source. The Chinese terminology (获取/模仿/理解应用/创造/进化 and their sub-levels) is the framework's own organizational vocabulary. The Stage 5 feedback loop structure (感知→认知→分析→理解→评估→反思) is a synthesis of Piaget's developmental cycle with Bloom's higher-order categories and is not directly quoted from either source.
