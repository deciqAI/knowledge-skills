# Sources — probabilistic-thinking

> *Primary sources for the [probabilistic-thinking](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **Bayes, Thomas.** "An Essay towards solving a Problem in the Doctrine of Chances." *Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society of London*, Vol. 53 (1763), pp. 370–418. Posthumously published by Richard Price. **The primary source** for Bayes' theorem. https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rstl.1763.0053
- **Knight, Frank H.** *Risk, Uncertainty, and Profit*. Houghton Mifflin, 1921. **Primary source** for the risk-vs-uncertainty distinction; ch. VII–VIII especially. Library of Economics and Liberty: https://www.econlib.org/library/Knight/knRUP.html
- **Tetlock, Philip E.** *Expert Political Judgment: How Good Is It? How Can We Know?* Princeton University Press, 2005 (revised 2017). The foundational empirical study on expert prediction accuracy.
- **Tetlock, Philip E. & Gardner, Dan.** *Superforecasting: The Art and Science of Prediction*. Crown, 2015. The synthesis of the Good Judgment Project findings.
- **Mellers, Barbara et al.** "Psychological Strategies for Winning a Geopolitical Forecasting Tournament." *Psychological Science*, Vol. 25, No. 5 (May 2014), pp. 1106–1115. The peer-reviewed evidence on trainability of calibration. https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0956797614524255
- **Kahneman, Daniel & Tversky, Amos.** "On the Psychology of Prediction." *Psychological Review*, Vol. 80, No. 4 (1973), pp. 237–251. The original base-rate-neglect demonstrations.
- **Gigerenzer, Gerd & Hoffrage, Ulrich.** "How to Improve Bayesian Reasoning Without Instruction: Frequency Formats." *Psychological Review*, Vol. 102, No. 4 (1995), pp. 684–704. On why representing probabilities as natural frequencies dramatically improves intuitive Bayesian reasoning.
- IARPA Aggregative Contingent Estimation (ACE) program documentation: https://www.iarpa.gov/research-programs/ace
- **Grace, Katja et al.** "Thousands of AI Authors on the Future of AI." AI Impacts, 2024 (survey of published ML researchers). Documents the wide dispersion and instability of expert AI-timeline forecasts — a contemporary illustration of why AGI timelines are proxy-reference-class / partly-Knightian, not actuarial. https://aiimpacts.org/
- **Metaculus — AI forecasting questions.** https://www.metaculus.com/questions/ . A public, resolution-criteria-based forecasting platform; its AGI/agentic-capability questions demonstrate how vague "when AGI?" claims must be operationalized (outcome + threshold + verifier + deadline) before they can be scored.
- The popular slogan "trust the data" is **not** cited as a source. Data alone, without a base rate and an update rule, is just numbers; the framework is what makes them probabilistic claims.
