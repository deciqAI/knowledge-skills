# Sources — middle-way

> *Primary sources for the [middle-way](../SKILL.md) skill.*

## Sources

- *Zhongyong* (中庸), attributed to Zisi (子思, c. 481-402 BCE). Translations include James Legge, *The Doctrine of the Mean* (1885); Wing-Tsit Chan, *A Source Book in Chinese Philosophy* (Princeton, 1963), ISBN 978-0691019642; Roger Ames & David Hall, *Focusing the Familiar: A Translation and Philosophical Interpretation of the Zhongyong* (Hawaii, 2001), ISBN 978-0824824600.
- Confucius (Kongzi, 孔子). *The Analects (Lunyu, 論語)*. Multiple translations; Edward Slingerland (Hackett, 2003), ISBN 978-0872206359, is the canonical modern academic translation.
- Zhu Xi (朱熹, 1130-1200). *Zhongyong Zhangju (中庸章句)*. The 12th-century commentary that elevated the Zhongyong to canonical status. Discussion in Daniel Gardner, *Zhu Xi's Reading of the Analects* (Columbia, 2003), ISBN 978-0231128636.
- Aristotle. *Nicomachean Ethics*, Book II. The Western parallel "doctrine of the mean." Translations including Terence Irwin (Hackett, 1999), ISBN 978-0872204645.
- Giddens, A. (1998). *The Third Way: The Renewal of Social Democracy.* Polity. ISBN 978-0745621333. The modern political application.
- Stiglitz, J. E. (2002). *Globalization and Its Discontents.* Norton. ISBN 978-0393324396. The economic-policy middle-way argument.
- Ames, R. T. & Rosemont, H. (1998). *The Analects of Confucius: A Philosophical Translation.* Ballantine. ISBN 978-0345434074. Especially their interpretive notes on *zhongyong*.
