# Sources — tragedy-of-the-commons

> *Primary sources for the [tragedy-of-the-commons](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Hardin, G. (1968). "The Tragedy of the Commons." *Science*, 162(3859), 1243-1248. The foundational paper.
- Hardin, G. (1998). "Extensions of 'The Tragedy of the Commons'." *Science*, 280(5364), 682-683. Hardin's update acknowledging Ostrom.
- Ostrom, E. (1990). *Governing the Commons: The Evolution of Institutions for Collective Action.* Cambridge University Press. ISBN 978-0521405997. The empirical correction.
- Ostrom, E. (2009). "A General Framework for Analyzing Sustainability of Social-Ecological Systems." *Science*, 325(5939), 419-422. The synthesis.
- Olson, M. (1965). *The Logic of Collective Action.* Harvard University Press. ISBN 978-0674537514. The collective-action problem foundation.
- Lloyd, W. F. (1833). *Two Lectures on the Checks to Population.* Oxford University Press. The historical predecessor Hardin cites.
- Dietz, T., Ostrom, E., & Stern, P. C. (2003). "The struggle to govern the commons." *Science*, 302(5652), 1907-1912. The state-of-the-art summary.
- Ostrom, E., Burger, J., Field, C. B., Norgaard, R. B., & Policansky, D. (1999). "Revisiting the commons: Local lessons, global challenges." *Science*, 284(5412), 278-282.
- Buck, S. J. (1998). *The Global Commons: An Introduction.* Island Press. ISBN 978-1559633499. Applied to modern global commons.
- Kurlansky, M. (1997). *Cod: A Biography of the Fish That Changed the World.* Walker & Company. The Grand Banks collapse narrative.
- Hutchings, J. A., & Myers, R. A. (1994). "What can be learned from the collapse of a renewable resource? Atlantic cod, Gadus morhua, of Newfoundland and Labrador." *Canadian Journal of Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences*, 51(9), 2126-2146. The scientific post-mortem of the cod collapse.
