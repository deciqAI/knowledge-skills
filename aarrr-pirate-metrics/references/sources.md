# Sources — aarrr-pirate-metrics

> *Primary sources for the [aarrr-pirate-metrics](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **McClure, Dave.** *Startup Metrics for Pirates* (AARRR). The framework was first presented in 2007 (Ignite Seattle); the widely-circulated "long version" slide deck linked here is the Startonomics SF edition (October 2008). SlideShare: https://www.slideshare.net/dmc500hats/startup-metrics-for-pirates-long-version **Canonical primary source for AARRR.**
- **Goldratt, Eliyahu M.** *The Goal: A Process of Ongoing Improvement*. North River Press, 1984; rev. 3rd ed. 2014. **The Theory of Constraints** that underpins the bottleneck logic of AARRR.
- **Ellis, Sean & Brown, Morgan.** *Hacking Growth: How Today's Fastest-Growing Companies Drive Breakout Success*. Crown Business, 2017. Growth-marketing case studies including the Dropbox referral case and Facebook's "7 friends in 10 days" activation metric.
- **Palihapitiya, Chamath.** "How We Put Facebook on the Path to 1 Billion Users" (public talk, c. 2012; widely circulated 2013). First-person account of Facebook's growth team and the "7 friends in 10 days" activation metric. Note: the "7 friends in 10 days" figure is a widely-repeated growth-team anecdote rather than a formally published metric; treat as illustrative.
- **Dropbox Inc., Form S-1 (SEC, 2018)** — primary-source user growth milestones: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1467623/000119312518055809/d451946ds1.htm
- **Sequoia Capital case studies on Dropbox** — primary-source venture history: https://www.sequoiacap.com/
- **OpenAI.** "Introducing ChatGPT" (November 30, 2022): https://openai.com/blog/chatgpt — dates the acquisition-side inflection for the 2023–2026 AI SaaS funnel example (AI-hype traffic makes acquisition cheap).
- **Sequoia Capital / Cahn, David.** "AI's $600B Question" (2024): https://www.sequoiacap.com/article/ais-600b-question/ — widely-cited analysis of the gap between AI infrastructure/capex build-out and durable end-application revenue and retention; context for why activation/retention (not acquisition) is the AI-SaaS bottleneck.
- The popular framing "growth hacking" is **not** cited as a source — by this skill's own rule, an aphorism is not evidence. The framework is precisely "instrument the funnel, find the bottleneck stage relative to benchmark, concentrate effort there, re-measure."
