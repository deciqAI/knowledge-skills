# Sources — aarrr-pirate-metrics

> *Primary sources for the [aarrr-pirate-metrics](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **McClure, Dave.** *Startup Metrics for Pirates* (slide deck, Startonomics SF, August 2007). Original SlideShare: https://www.slideshare.net/dmc500hats/startup-metrics-for-pirates-long-version **Canonical primary source for AARRR.**
- **Goldratt, Eliyahu M.** *The Goal: A Process of Ongoing Improvement*. North River Press, 1984; rev. 3rd ed. 2014. **The Theory of Constraints** that underpins the bottleneck logic of AARRR.
- **Ellis, Sean & Brown, Morgan.** *Hacking Growth: How Today's Fastest-Growing Companies Drive Breakout Success*. Crown Business, 2017. Growth-marketing case studies including the Dropbox referral case and Facebook's "7 friends in 10 days" activation metric.
- **Palihapitiya, Chamath.** "How We Put Facebook on the Path to a Billion Users" (public talk, 2013). Primary first-person account of Facebook's growth team and the "7 friends in 10 days" north-star activation metric.
- **Dropbox Inc., Form S-1 (SEC, 2018)** — primary-source user growth milestones: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1467623/000119312518055809/d451946ds1.htm
- **Sequoia Capital case studies on Dropbox** — primary-source venture history: https://www.sequoiacap.com/
- The popular framing "growth hacking" is **not** cited as a source — by this skill's own rule, an aphorism is not evidence. The framework is precisely "instrument the funnel, find the bottleneck stage relative to benchmark, concentrate effort there, re-measure."
