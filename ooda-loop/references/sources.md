# Sources — ooda-loop

> *Primary sources for the [ooda-loop](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Boyd, J. R. (1976-1995). "Patterns of Conflict" briefing slides. Unpublished primary source; reproduced in multiple subsequent works.
- Coram, R. (2002). *Boyd: The Fighter Pilot Who Changed the Art of War.* Little, Brown. ISBN 978-0316796880. The accessible biographical treatment with extensive coverage of Korean War analysis.
- Osinga, F. P. B. (2007). *Science, Strategy and War: The Strategic Theory of John Boyd.* Routledge. ISBN 978-0415459525. The canonical scholarly source on Boyd's strategic theory.
- Hammond, G. T. (2001). *The Mind of War: John Boyd and American Security.* Smithsonian Institution Press. ISBN 978-1588340788. A second comprehensive treatment.
- US Marine Corps (1989/1997). *Warfighting* (MCDP 1). The doctrinal manual that integrates Boyd's framework into modern military doctrine.
- Richards, C. W. (2004). *Certain to Win: The Strategy of John Boyd, Applied to Business.* Xlibris. ISBN 978-1413449037. Business-application synthesis.
- Stanford HAI (2024, 2025). *Artificial Intelligence Index Report.* Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI. https://aiindex.stanford.edu — tracks frontier-model release frequency, capability trends, and the growth of open-weight models; background for the 2024–2026 AI-competition example.
- Frontier-lab public release announcements (2023–2025): OpenAI (openai.com/blog), Anthropic (anthropic.com/news), and Google DeepMind (deepmind.google) model-release pages — primary, durable documentation of the accelerating release cadence referenced in the 2024–2026 example.
