# Sources — scenario-planning

> *Primary sources for the [scenario-planning](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **Wack, Pierre.** "Scenarios: Uncharted Waters Ahead." *Harvard Business Review*, Vol. 63, No. 5 (September–October 1985), pp. 73–89. **Primary source** for Shell's scenario practice and the 1973 oil crisis case, written by the practitioner who ran the exercise. https://hbr.org/1985/09/scenarios-uncharted-waters-ahead
- **Wack, Pierre.** "Scenarios: Shooting the Rapids." *Harvard Business Review*, Vol. 63, No. 6 (November–December 1985), pp. 139–150. The companion piece on translating scenarios into managerial action — addresses the persuasion bottleneck directly. https://hbr.org/1985/11/scenarios-shooting-the-rapids
- **Schwartz, Peter.** *The Art of the Long View: Planning for the Future in an Uncertain World.* Currency/Doubleday, 1991. The codification of the GBN scenario method; the operational manual that made the practice portable. Verbatim quote above from p. 9.
- **Kahn, Herman & Wiener, Anthony J.** *The Year 2000: A Framework for Speculation on the Next Thirty-Three Years.* Macmillan, 1967. The originating military-to-civilian transfer of scenario methodology; establishes the intellectual lineage.
- **van der Heijden, Kees.** *Scenarios: The Art of Strategic Conversation.* Wiley, 1996. The academic treatment of scenario planning as organizational learning, grounding the practice in epistemology.
- **U.S. National Intelligence Council.** *Global Trends 2040: A More Contested World.* Office of the Director of National Intelligence, 2021. A publicly available example of institutional scenario planning at scale; demonstrates the methodology applied to 20-year horizons. https://www.dni.gov/index.php/gt2040-home
- The phrase "plan for the future" is **not** cited as a source — planning intent without a stated method is not scenario planning. The distinction between *scenario planning* (multiple internally consistent futures) and *forecasting* (single probabilistic estimate) is the entire point; popular usage conflates them.
