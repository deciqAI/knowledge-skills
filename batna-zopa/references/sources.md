# Sources — batna-zopa

> *Primary sources for the [batna-zopa](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Fisher, R., & Ury, W. (1981/1991). *Getting to Yes: Negotiating Agreement Without Giving In* (2nd ed.). Penguin. ISBN 978-0140157352. The founding text; BATNA defined on pp. 99-108.
- Lax, D. A., & Sebenius, J. K. (1986). *The Manager as Negotiator.* Free Press. The first systematic extension into business negotiation. ISBN 978-0029187706.
- Quandt, W. B. (1986). *Camp David: Peacemaking and Politics.* Brookings Institution. The definitive scholarly account of the 1978 Camp David negotiations — the framework's canonical diplomatic case.
- Mnookin, R. H. (2000). *Beyond Winning: Negotiating to Create Value in Deals and Disputes.* Harvard University Press. The framework applied to legal and business disputes. ISBN 978-0674008830.
- Malhotra, D., & Bazerman, M. (2007). *Negotiation Genius: How to Overcome Obstacles and Achieve Brilliant Results at the Bargaining Table and Beyond.* Bantam. Modern operational synthesis. ISBN 978-0553804881.
- Harvard Program on Negotiation: https://www.pon.harvard.edu/ — continues active research and case-study development.
