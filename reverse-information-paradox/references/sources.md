# Sources — reverse-information-paradox

> *Primary sources for the [reverse-information-paradox](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Note on maturity:** The Reverse Information Paradox is a **July-2026, single-origin thesis** — coined by Satya Nadella and, at authoring time, covered only by contemporaneous press and commentary. It is presented throughout as a contemporary strategic argument, explicitly dated, **not** as time-tested empirical canon. Its intellectual parent, Arrow's information paradox, is the established concept it inverts.

- **Satya Nadella (Microsoft Chairman & CEO), "Reverse Information Paradox" strategy essay — published 2026-07-12.** Coins the term and inverts Arrow's paradox onto the AI *buyer*: to make external AI useful you must reveal proprietary knowledge, leaving "intelligence exhaust" (prompts, tool calls, corrections, evals) — described in coverage of the thesis as a record of how an organization works and makes decisions — that flows to whoever controls the learning infrastructure. Prescription: a hard trust boundary inside each enterprise tenant (own your data, evals, memory, adapted weights, learning loops) and an orchestration layer decoupled from any single model provider. Verbatim quotes used in this skill:
  - *"you essentially pay for intelligence twice … once with money, and again with something even more valuable: the proprietary knowledge you must reveal to make that intelligence useful."*
  - *"In consuming intelligence, you are creating intelligence. And what you create should belong to you."*
  - *"The better you want the model to perform, the more of that knowledge you have to feed it."*
- **MIT Sloan Management Review Middle East — coverage of Nadella's Reverse Information Paradox (July 2026).** Reputable management-press write-up of the thesis and its trust-boundary prescription. https://mitsloanme.com
- **ANI News — coverage dated 2026-07-13.** News-wire report of Nadella's essay and the "pay twice" framing. https://www.aninews.in
- **Trending Topics EU — coverage of the Reverse Information Paradox (July 2026).** Independent European tech-press treatment of the "intelligence exhaust" construct and the decoupled-orchestration recommendation. https://www.trendingtopics.eu
- **PPC Land — coverage of the Reverse Information Paradox (July 2026).** Trade-press write-up of the thesis and its implications for AI adoption. https://ppc.land
- **Kenneth J. Arrow (1962), "Economic Welfare and the Allocation of Resources for Invention," in *The Rate and Direction of Inventive Activity: Economic and Social Factors*, Princeton University Press.** The parent concept the 2026 thesis inverts: the classic (seller-side) information paradox — a buyer cannot value information without seeing it, and once seen has no need to pay. https://www.nber.org/books-and-chapters/rate-and-direction-inventive-activity-economic-and-social-factors
