# Sources — tunnel-vision-slack

> *Primary sources for the [tunnel-vision-slack](../SKILL.md) skill.*

1. **Mullainathan, Sendhil & Shafir, Eldar. *Scarcity: Why Having Too Little Means So Much.* Henry Holt, 2013.** https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780805092646/scarcity
   > "Scarcity captures the mind. When we experience scarcity of any kind, we become absorbed by it… This is the scarcity trap: the very nature of scarcity… changes how we think in ways that further perpetuate scarcity."
   The foundational empirical and conceptual treatment of bandwidth scarcity, tunneling, and the scarcity trap. Provides the cognitive mechanism underlying the tunnel vision effect.

2. **Simon, Herbert A. "Designing Organizations for an Information-Rich World." In *Computers, Communications, and the Public Interest*, ed. Martin Greenberger. Johns Hopkins University Press, 1971.** https://is.ite.uni-bonn.de/web_course_docs/e-business/ss03/seminar/Material/Simon1971.pdf
   > "A wealth of information creates a poverty of attention and a need to allocate that attention efficiently among the overabundance of information sources that might consume it."
   Simon's attention-economics framing establishes why the tunnel is structural (information overabundance creates attention scarcity) rather than motivational.

3. **Eisenhower, D. "Address at the Second Assembly of the World Council of Churches" (1954).** Eisenhower Presidential Library. https://www.eisenhowerlibrary.gov/research/online-documents
   > "I have two kinds of problems, the urgent and the important. The urgent are not important, and the important are never urgent."
   Primary-source documentation of the structural slack principle in practice.

**What is NOT cited and why:** Malcolm Gladwell's popularization of the tunnel metaphor in *Outliers* (2008) is omitted because it is a secondary synthesis without the mechanistic rigor of Mullainathan/Shafir. Stephen Covey's *7 Habits* (Quadrant II concept) is omitted because it is a self-help frame without the scarcity-mechanism explanation that makes the cognitive trap comprehensible. Inclusion would dilute the primary-source standard.
