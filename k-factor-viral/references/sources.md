# Sources — k-factor-viral

> *Primary sources for the [k-factor-viral](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Jurvetson, S. & Draper, T. (December 1996/May 2000). "Viral Marketing." *Netscape M-Files* / *Red Herring Magazine*. The originating articulation of "viral marketing" in the tech industry. Coverage at https://www.dfj.com/ and reproduced widely.
- Skok, D. (2009-2014). "Viral Marketing — The Science Of Sharing." *For Entrepreneurs* blog. https://www.forentrepreneurs.com/lessons-learnt-viral-marketing/ — the most operationally detailed treatment of the K = i × c decomposition for SaaS.
- Chen, A. (2009-2022). Multiple posts on growth and virality, especially "What's your viral loop? Understanding the engine of adoption" and "When network effects fail." Collected at https://andrewchen.com/
- Bhatia, S. (interviews and recollections of Hotmail's growth). See e.g. *Inc. Magazine* interview, "Hotmail's Sabeer Bhatia on Selling to Microsoft," and Bronson, P. (2000). *The Nudist on the Late Shift.* Random House, ISBN 978-0375502774, which contains detailed Hotmail history.
- Ries, E. (2011). *The Lean Startup.* Crown Business. ISBN 978-0307887894. The "Engines of Growth" chapter formalizes viral (K-driven) growth alongside sticky and paid engines.
- Bass, F. M. (1969). "A new product growth model for consumer durables." *Management Science*, 15(5), 215-227. The mathematical ancestor of modern viral-growth modeling.
