# Sources — economies-of-scale

> *Primary sources for the [economies-of-scale](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Smith, A. (1776). *An Inquiry into the Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations.* W. Strahan. Book I, Ch. 1. The original pin factory demonstration.
- Marshall, A. (1890). *Principles of Economics.* Macmillan. Book IV, Ch. 9–13. Internal vs. external economies; foundational analytical framework.
- Krugman, P. (1979). "Increasing returns, monopolistic competition, and international trade." *Journal of International Economics*, 9(4), 469–479. Nobel Prize-winning formalization.
- Varian, H. R. (1992). *Microeconomic Analysis.* 3rd ed. Norton. ISBN 978-0393957358. Standard graduate treatment of scale economies and cost curves.
- Chandler, A. D. (1990). *Scale and Scope: The Dynamics of Industrial Capitalism.* Belknap. ISBN 978-0674789951. Historical study of how scale shaped 20th-century industrial structure.
- Hounshell, D. A. (1984). *From the American System to Mass Production, 1800–1932: The Development of Manufacturing Technology in the United States.* Johns Hopkins University Press. Definitive archival history of Ford's Highland Park assembly line and the Model T scale curve.
- Ford, H., with Crowther, S. (1922). *My Life and Work.* Doubleday, Page & Company. Ford's own account of the price-first, cost-follows strategy.
- Porter, M. E. (1985). *Competitive Advantage.* Free Press. ISBN 978-0684841465. Cost leadership as one of the three generic strategies; scale as source of competitive advantage.
- The White House / U.S. Department of Commerce. *CHIPS and Science Act of 2022* (signed August 9, 2022); U.S. Bureau of Industry and Security, advanced-computing and semiconductor manufacturing export controls (initial rules October 7, 2022, subsequently tightened). Government intervention driven by minimum-efficient-scale concentration in leading-edge fabs.
- ASML Holding N.V. investor relations and public reporting on EUV / high-NA lithography systems and ASML's position as the sole supplier of EUV scanners; TSMC investor relations and public reporting on advanced-node leadership, capex, and the Arizona (Phoenix) fab. Contemporary (2024–2026) illustration of capital intensity, learning-curve/yield economies, and external (cluster) economies in leading-edge semiconductor manufacturing.
