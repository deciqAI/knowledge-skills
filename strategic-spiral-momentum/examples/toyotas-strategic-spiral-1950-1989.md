# Method in Action: Toyota's Strategic Spiral (1950–1989)

> *Example for the [strategic-spiral-momentum](../SKILL.md) skill.*

Toyota's rise from near-bankruptcy to global automotive leadership is a documented 10-phase spiral executed over approximately 35 years.

**Phase 1 (谋势/筑基, 1950):** Eiji Toyoda visited Ford's River Rouge plant in 1950. He returned with a strategic direction: adapt mass production principles to Toyota's severe capital constraints. The foundational capability target was clear — build quality without the inventory Ford could afford.

**Phases 2–3 (蓄势+造势/开局+验证, 1950–1965):** Taiichi Ohno developed and internally validated the Toyota Production System (TPS). This was a 15-year 蓄势 — accumulating methodological capability before market-facing claims. External validation came only after internal proof.

**Phases 4–5 (借势+起势/占位+加速, 1965–1975):** Toyota leveraged the 1973 oil shock as the macro tailwind that made TPS's fuel efficiency a decisive competitive advantage. This is the canonical 借势 example — the tailwind was not created by Toyota, but Toyota was positioned to capture it because of the prior 蓄势.

**Phases 6–7 (乘势+定势, 1975–1982):** Toyota rode the wave as the dominant quality/efficiency producer. Market share in North America grew from 1.8% (1971) to 6.5% (1981). 定势 activities: formalizing supplier relationships (keiretsu), establishing the Toyota Way as a teachable doctrine.

**Phases 8–9 (化势+应势/系统+再谋, 1982–1986):** TPS was converted from an internal practice into a systematic, transferable methodology via the publication of Ohno's *Toyota Production System* (English translation, 1988). Simultaneously, leadership was reading next-cycle signals: the North American market was demanding luxury vehicles Toyota could not yet address.

**Phase 10 (换势/重启, 1989):** Launch of Lexus — a restart at a higher dimension, entering a new market segment with a new brand, new supply chain relationships, and new capability. This was not iteration; it was spiral restart.

Sources: Womack, J.P., Jones, D.T. & Roos, D. *The Machine That Changed the World* (Free Press, 1990); Ohno, T. *Toyota Production System* (English trans. Productivity Press, 1988) https://www.productivitypress.com/toyota-production-system.html
