# Sources — strategic-spiral-momentum

> *Primary sources for the [strategic-spiral-momentum](../SKILL.md) skill.*

1. **Sun Tzu, *The Art of War*** (c. 500 BC). https://ctext.org/art-of-war — Chapter 6 on water/ground adaptability provides the foundational logic for cyclical momentum: form (形) gives way to momentum (势) and back, in an endless adaptive cycle. Verbatim: "Water shapes its course according to the nature of the ground over which it flows; the soldier works out his victory in relation to the foe whom he is facing. Therefore, just as water retains no constant shape, so in warfare there are no constant conditions."

2. **Peter Senge, *The Fifth Discipline*** (1990). https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/316158/the-fifth-discipline-by-peter-m-senge/ — Systems thinking as the basis for seeing strategic cycles as circles of influence rather than linear chains. Verbatim: "The key to seeing reality systemically is seeing circles of influence rather than straight lines... Every influence is both cause and effect. Nothing is ever influenced in just one direction."

3. **Womack, J.P., Jones, D.T. & Roos, D. *The Machine That Changed the World*** (Free Press, 1990) — Primary source on Toyota's strategic evolution; documents the TPS development timeline and the competitive dynamics of the 1970s automotive market. Used for the Method in Action section.

4. **Ohno, T. *Toyota Production System*** (English trans. Productivity Press, 1988). https://www.productivitypress.com/toyota-production-system.html — Ohno's first-person account of the 蓄势 phase; documents the 15-year internal validation period before external market claims.

**What was NOT cited and why:** Peter Drucker's *Management* and *Innovation and Entrepreneurship* contain related thinking on organizational cycles, but are not cited here because they do not address the spiral/restart mechanism specifically — the Senge citation covers the systems-thinking foundation more precisely. Pop-management figures such as Jim Collins (*Good to Great*) are not cited because the primary sources above are more specific to the spiral mechanism and more verifiable.
