# Sources — decision-tree

> *Primary sources for the [decision-tree](../SKILL.md) skill.*

## Sources

- Magee, J. F. (1964). "Decision Trees for Decision Making." *Harvard Business Review*, July–August, 126–138. The original practitioner framework.
- Magee, J. F. (1964). "How to Use Decision Trees in Capital Investment." *Harvard Business Review*, September–October, 79–96. Capital investment application.
- Raiffa, H. (1968). *Decision Analysis: Introductory Lectures on Choices under Uncertainty.* Addison-Wesley. The academic formalization; introduces EVPI and formal rollback.
- Howard, R. A. (1966). "Decision Analysis: Applied Decision Theory." *Proceedings of the 4th International Conference on Operational Research.* Stanford. Early theoretical grounding.
- Howard, R. A., & Matheson, J. E. (Eds.) (1983). *Readings on the Principles and Applications of Decision Analysis.* Strategic Decisions Group. Comprehensive practitioner reference.
- Clemen, R. T., & Reilly, T. (2013). *Making Hard Decisions with DecisionTools.* Cengage. Standard textbook treatment.
- Eisenhower, D. D. (1948). *Crusade in Europe.* Doubleday. First-hand account of the D-Day weather go/no-go decision (worked example).
- Stagg, J. M. (1971). *Forecast for Overlord.* Ian Allan. The SHAEF chief meteorologist's account of the June 1944 forecasts.
- Major U.S. hyperscaler quarterly earnings guidance and calls, 2024–2026 (Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta). Primary source for the reported surge in AI-related capital expenditure and management's commentary on committing to AI capacity under uncertain returns. Context for the AI build-vs-buy-vs-wait worked example.
- Mainstream financial-press coverage of the 2024–2026 "AI capex / overbuild / bubble" debate (e.g., The Wall Street Journal, Financial Times, The Economist, Reuters). Durable, widely-reported context for how large firms weighed heavy AI infrastructure investment against the risk of stranded capacity.
- Leading semiconductor foundries' and integrated device makers' public capital-expenditure guidance and management commentary, 2024–2026 (as reported in earnings materials and mainstream financial press). Context for the chipmaker leading-edge-fab worked example: the multi-year, multi-billion-dollar nature of advanced-node fab commitments made ahead of confirmed AI-driven demand.
- Widely reported industry coverage of the semiconductor sector's historical boom-bust capacity cycles and of the 2024–2026 debate over whether AI-driven demand for leading-edge chips is durable or a potential overbuild (e.g., trade press such as SemiAnalysis-style analysis alongside general financial press). Durable, well-documented context for the high/low-demand probability framing; no specific figures in the worked example are drawn from any single filing.
