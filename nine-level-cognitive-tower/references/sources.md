# Sources — nine-level-cognitive-tower

> *Primary sources for the [nine-level-cognitive-tower](../SKILL.md) skill.*

## Sources

1. Bloom, B.S., Engelhart, M.D., Furst, E.J., Hill, W.H., & Krathwohl, D.R. (1956). *Taxonomy of Educational Objectives: The Classification of Educational Goals, Handbook I: Cognitive Domain.* David McKay Company. https://www.uky.edu/~rsand1/china2012/texts/Bloom%20et%20al%201956%20Taxonomy.pdf
   > "The taxonomy... represents a classification of the goals of our educational system... The six major classes are: Knowledge, Comprehension, Application, Analysis, Synthesis, Evaluation."

2. Confucius. *Analects* (c. 500 BC), Book 2, Chapter 4. Trans. James Legge. https://ctext.org/analects/wei-zheng
   > "At fifteen, I had my mind bent on learning. At thirty, I stood firm. At forty, I had no doubts. At fifty, I knew the decrees of Heaven. At sixty, my ear was an obedient organ. At seventy, I could follow what my heart desired, without transgressing what was right."

3. Franklin, B. (1791). *The Autobiography of Benjamin Franklin*, Part 2. Project Gutenberg. https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/20203
   > "I was surpris'd to find myself so much fuller of Faults than I had imagined; but I had the Satisfaction of seeing them diminish."

**What is NOT cited and why:** The specific nine-level structure (九层塔) as presented in this skill is a synthesis derived from Bloom's taxonomy and Confucian developmental philosophy, not a direct quote from a single primary source. The nine levels are a framework adaptation, not an empirically validated psychological scale. Bloom's taxonomy has been revised (Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001) and the original six-level version has known limitations; this framework uses the spirit of progressive cognitive development rather than claiming equivalence with the revised taxonomy.
