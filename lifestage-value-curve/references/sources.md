# Sources — lifestage-value-curve

> *Primary sources for the [lifestage-value-curve](../SKILL.md) skill.*

1. Hesiod. *Works and Days* (c. 700 BC), lines 109-201. Trans. Hugh G. Evelyn-White (1914). Loeb Classical Library. https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:1999.01.0132
   > "First of all the deathless gods who dwell on Olympus made a golden race of mortal men... like gods they lived with hearts free from all grief and toil and sorrow... Afterwards, they who dwell on Olympus made a second generation which was of silver and less noble by far than the golden."

2. Erikson, E.H. (1950). *Childhood and Society*. W.W. Norton. https://archive.org/details/childhoodsociety00erik
   > "Generativity is primarily the concern in establishing and guiding the next generation... The mere fact of having or even wanting children... does not 'achieve' generativity."

3. Darwin, C. (1887). *The Autobiography of Charles Darwin*, ed. Francis Darwin. John Murray. https://www.darwinproject.ac.uk/
   > "I worked on true Baconian principles, and without any theory collected facts on a wholesale scale."

**What is NOT cited and why:** The seven specific stage names (蛮荒时代, 启蒙时代, 英雄时代, 黄金时代, 白银时代, 青铜时代, 第二蛮荒时代) as mapped to specific age ranges are a framework synthesis, not a direct transcription of Hesiod or Erikson. Hesiod's five ages are mythological and do not map to specific years. Erikson's eight stages use different boundaries. The age ranges used here are practical defaults derived from observable career patterns, not empirically validated psychological stages. Users should treat the age ranges as strong starting hypotheses, not as validated developmental milestones.
