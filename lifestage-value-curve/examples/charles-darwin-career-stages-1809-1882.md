# Method in Action: Charles Darwin's Career Stages (1809-1882)

> *Example for the [lifestage-value-curve](../SKILL.md) skill.*

Darwin's career provides one of the most documented examples of stage-specific investment patterns producing stage-specific value.

**Enlightenment Age (20s):** Darwin spent his early career in maximum curiosity and learning mode — studying medicine briefly (Edinburgh), then theology (Cambridge), building relationships with mentors like John Stevens Henslow, and committing to natural history as his domain of deepest investment.

**Heroic Age (late 20s-30s):** The Beagle voyage (1831-1836) was precisely a Heroic Age investment — maximum energy deployed in the field, building the raw empirical foundation for everything that followed. Darwin collected specimens, made observations, took risks (health, reputation), and built his track record. He did not try to synthesize at this stage; he invested in the raw material.

**Golden Age (40s):** By his 40s, Darwin had 20 years of accumulated observations, specimens, and correspondence. He spent this decade in synthesis. He wrote, revised, corresponded with the world's naturalists, and structured his argument. *On the Origin of Species* was published in 1859, when Darwin was 50 — at the very peak of the Golden Age window. The value was not in the energy of collection (Heroic Age) but in the synthesis of everything the Heroic Age had built.

> "I worked on true Baconian principles, and without any theory collected facts on a wholesale scale."
> — Darwin, C. (1887). *The Autobiography of Charles Darwin*, ed. Francis Darwin. John Murray. https://www.darwinproject.ac.uk/

The Heroic Age built the raw material. The Golden Age synthesized it. Without the Heroic Age investment, the Golden Age had nothing to synthesize. Without the Golden Age synthesis, the Heroic Age collection was unpublished data.

**Silver Age (50s-60s):** Darwin produced subsequent works — *The Descent of Man* (1871), *The Expression of the Emotions* (1872) — in the Silver Age mode: deeper wisdom, selective focus, drawing on the Golden Age synthesis to extend into adjacent domains.

Primary source: Darwin, C. (1859). *On the Origin of Species*. John Murray. https://www.darwinproject.ac.uk/
