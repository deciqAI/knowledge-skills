# Sources — black-swan

> *Primary sources for the [black-swan](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Taleb, N. N. (2007). *The Black Swan: The Impact of the Highly Improbable.* Random House. ISBN 978-1400063512. The founding text.
- Taleb, N. N. (2001). *Fooled by Randomness: The Hidden Role of Chance in Life and in the Markets.* Random House. ISBN 978-0812975215. The precursor on distinguishing skill from luck.
- Lowenstein, R. (2000). *When Genius Failed: The Rise and Fall of Long-Term Capital Management.* Random House. ISBN 978-0375758256. The journalistic post-mortem of LTCM.
- Jorion, P. (2000). "Risk Management Lessons from Long-Term Capital Management." *European Financial Management*, 6(3), 277-300. The academic analysis of LTCM's risk-model failures.
- Mandelbrot, B. B. (1963). "The Variation of Certain Speculative Prices." *Journal of Business*, 36(4), 394-419. The foundational mathematical paper showing financial returns are not Gaussian.
- The National Diet of Japan (2012). *The Official Report of the Fukushima Nuclear Accident Independent Investigation Commission (NAIIC).* National Diet of Japan, Tokyo. Chaired by Kiyoshi Kurokawa; concluded the disaster was foreseeable and preventable — a grey swan mislabeled black.
- Minoura, K., Imamura, F., Sugawara, D., Kono, Y., Iwashita, T. (2001). "The 869 Jōgan tsunami deposit and recurrence interval of large-scale tsunami on the Pacific coast of northeast Japan." *Journal of Natural Disaster Science*, 23(2), 83-88. The paleotsunami evidence available before 2011.
