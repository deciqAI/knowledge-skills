# Sources — thucydides-trap

> *Primary sources for the [thucydides-trap](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **Thucydides.** *History of the Peloponnesian War* (~400 BCE). Translated by Rex Warner. Penguin Classics, 1972. **Primary source for the foundational observation.** The verbatim Overview quote is from Book I, ch. 23. https://archive.org/details/historyofpelop00thuc
- **Allison, Graham.** *Destined for War: Can America and China Escape Thucydides's Trap?* Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2017. **Primary source for the 16-case historical dataset, the 12/16 war statistic, and the four escape path framework.** Verbatim Overview quote from ch. 1. https://www.houghtonmifflinbooks.com/titles/destined-for-war/
- **Allison, Graham.** "The Thucydides Trap: Are the U.S. and China Headed for War?" *The Atlantic*, September 24, 2015. **First public presentation of the 16-case dataset.** https://www.theatlantic.com/international/archive/2015/09/united-states-china-war-thucydides-trap/406756/
- **Harvard Kennedy School, Belfer Center.** *Thucydides's Trap Case File.* Ongoing research project documenting all 16 cases with primary sources. https://www.belfercenter.org/thucydides-trap/overview
- **Kennedy, Paul.** *The Rise and Fall of the Great Powers.* Random House, 1987. Historical context for great-power transitions and the structural economic and military dynamics Allison's framework builds upon.

**Not cited and why:** Popular media summaries of the US-China rivalry that cite "Thucydides's Trap" as a definitive prediction of war are using it as prophecy rather than structural analysis — this skill uses it as a diagnostic tool with a historical base rate and specific escape path criteria. The claim that "nuclear weapons make the Trap obsolete" appears in commentary but lacks systematic historical analysis of nuclear-era great-power dynamics comparable to Allison's dataset.
