# Sources — regret-minimization

> *Primary sources for the [regret-minimization](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **Bezos, Jeff.** "1997 Letter to Shareholders," Amazon SEC filing — the public articulation of long-horizon mindset that underlies the framework's commitment step. https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1018724/000119312513151836/d511111dex991.htm
- **Bezos, Jeff.** 2010 Princeton Baccalaureate address, *We are what we choose*: https://www.princeton.edu/news/2010/05/30/2010-baccalaureate-remarks — the most accessible primary-source description of the framework's age-80 evaluator.
- **Stone, Brad.** *The Everything Store: Jeff Bezos and the Age of Amazon* (Little, Brown, 2013), chapters 1–2 — the 1994 D.E. Shaw → Amazon decision in detail; contains the verbatim Bezos quote on regret minimization quoted in the Overview and Method in Action sections.
- **Loomes, Graham & Sugden, Robert.** "Regret Theory: An Alternative Theory of Rational Choice Under Uncertainty," *The Economic Journal*, Vol. 92, No. 368 (Dec. 1982), pp. 805–824. The formal academic foundation: a decision theory in which the disutility of the path not taken is built in as an axiom. JSTOR: https://www.jstor.org/stable/2232669
- **Seneca, Lucius Annaeus.** *Epistulae Morales ad Lucilium*, Letters 24 and 91 — the Stoic practice of *Premeditatio Malorum* (premeditation of evils). Loeb Classical Library translation by Richard Mott Gummere; the verbatim passage in the Overview is from Letter 91.
- The popular framing "live with no regrets" is **not** cited here as a source — by this skill's own rule, an aphorism circulating without primary attribution is not evidence. The framework is the opposite of "no regrets" — it names regrets explicitly in order to pick the most livable one.
