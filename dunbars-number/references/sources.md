# Sources — dunbars-number

> *Primary sources for the [dunbars-number](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Dunbar, R. I. M. (1992). "Neocortex size as a constraint on group size in primates." *Journal of Human Evolution*, 22(6), 469–493. The original empirical paper.
- Dunbar, R. I. M. (2010). *How Many Friends Does One Person Need?* Harvard University Press. ISBN 978-0674057166. Accessible treatment with organizational extensions.
- Gladwell, M. (2000). *The Tipping Point.* Little, Brown. ISBN 978-0316346627. Chapter 5: Gore case + Dunbar number in organizational design.
- Dunbar, R. I. M. (1998). "The social brain hypothesis." *Evolutionary Anthropology*, 6(5), 178–190. The social brain hypothesis underpinning the neocortex constraint.
- Hill, R. A., & Dunbar, R. I. M. (2003). "Social network size in humans." *Human Nature*, 14(1), 53–72. Empirical validation of the 150 figure via social network analysis.
- Gore, W. L. "The Lattice Organization — A Philosophy of Enterprise." Internal document distributed to Gore associates (circa 1976). The Gore founding philosophy. (Not a formally published journal article; the widely cited secondary source for Gore's ~150 plant cap is Gladwell 2000, Ch. 5, above.)
- Dunbar, R. I. M. (2021). "Dunbar's number: why my theory that humans can only maintain 150 friendships has withstood 30 years of scrutiny." *The Conversation*, May 2021. Dunbar's own 30-year restatement — the limit is a cognitive/neocortical constraint, not one technology (social media, collaboration tools, or AI) can raise.
- On the 2023–2026 AI funding-and-hiring environment: contemporaneous, widely reported venture-capital concentration in AI and rapid headcount growth at leading AI-native companies (e.g., ongoing press coverage of OpenAI and Anthropic scaling). Cited as a well-documented general pattern for the "capital compresses the timeline to each Dunbar breakpoint" claim — not as any single unverified figure.
