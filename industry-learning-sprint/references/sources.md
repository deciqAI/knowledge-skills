# Sources — industry-learning-sprint

> *Primary sources for the [industry-learning-sprint](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary sources with verbatim quotes:**

1. Graham, B. & Dodd, D. *Security Analysis*. McGraw-Hill, 1934. https://www.amazon.com/Security-Analysis-Foreword-Buffett-Editions/dp/0071592539
   > "The object of security analysis is to present the important facts in a form that will enable an intelligent investor to form his own judgment."

2. Graham, B. *The Intelligent Investor*. Harper & Brothers, 1949.
   > "In the short run, the market is a voting machine but in the long run, it is a weighing machine."

**What is NOT cited and why:**

- Porter, M. *Competitive Strategy* (1980) is relevant to industry structure analysis but describes long-run structural positioning, not the rapid mental model formation that the sprint addresses. Porter's Five Forces is a useful lens after a sprint, not a substitute for one.
- Industry analyst reports (McKinsey, Bain, Goldman Sachs sector research) are deliberately excluded as primary sources because they represent the consensus view that the sprint is designed to think against, not from.
