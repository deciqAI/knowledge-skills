# Sources — anchoring

> *Primary sources for the [anchoring](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Tversky, A., & Kahneman, D. (1974). "Judgment under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases." *Science*, 185(4157), 1124–1131. The founding paper. The wheel-of-fortune experiment (p. 1128); the multiplication-order experiment; the statement that payoffs for accuracy did not reduce the anchoring effect. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.185.4157.1124
- Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow*. Farrar, Straus and Giroux. Chapter 11 is the most accessible synthesis of the anchoring literature and Kahneman's own retrospective on the 1974 finding. ISBN 978-0374275631.
- Northcraft, G. B., & Neale, M. A. (1987). "Experts, Amateurs, and Real Estate: An Anchoring-and-Adjustment Perspective on Property Pricing Decisions." *Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes*, 39(1), 84–97. Experienced real-estate agents shown to be anchored by listing prices even while denying it. https://doi.org/10.1016/0749-5978(87)90046-X
- Englich, B., Mussweiler, T., & Strack, F. (2006). "Playing Dice with Criminal Sentences: The Influence of Irrelevant Anchors on Experts' Judicial Decision Making." *Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin*, 32(2), 188–200. Judges' sentences anchored by literally random dice rolls. https://doi.org/10.1177/0146167205282152
- Galinsky, A. D., & Mussweiler, T. (2001). "First Offers as Anchors: The Role of Perspective-Taking and Negotiator Focus." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, 81(4), 657–669. The negotiation research showing that bold opening offers produce better outcomes. https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.81.4.657
- Mussweiler, T., Strack, F., & Pfeiffer, T. (2000). "Overcoming the Inevitable Anchoring Effect: Considering the Opposite Compensates for Selective Accessibility." *Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin*, 26(9), 1142–1150. "Consider the opposite" as a measurable but partial de-anchoring procedure. https://doi.org/10.1177/01461672002611010
- Ariely, D., Loewenstein, G., & Prelec, D. (2003). "Coherent Arbitrariness: Stable Demand Curves without Stable Preferences." *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 118(1), 73–106. The Social-Security-number auction experiment — random anchors influence bidding by multiples. https://doi.org/10.1162/00335530360535153
- Adam, H., & Shirako, A. (2013). "Not All Anger Is Created Equal: The Impact of the Expresser's Culture on the Social Effects of Anger in Negotiations." *Journal of Applied Psychology*, 98(5), 785–798. Cultural and contextual moderators of anchoring effects in negotiation. https://doi.org/10.1037/a0032387
- NVIDIA Corporation SEC filings (2023–2025). Primary-source quarterly and annual financials underlying the "Nvidia market cap as anchor" example; use for verifiable revenue/valuation data rather than headline figures. https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=NVDA
- Contemporary business-press coverage of the 2023–2026 AI funding cycle (Reuters, The Wall Street Journal, Bloomberg, Financial Times, The Information). Basis for the AI-fundraising anchoring example — Nvidia's 2023 crossing of a $1T market cap and 2024 rise among the most valuable public companies, and the large, rising reported OpenAI/Anthropic funding rounds across 2023–2025. Exact valuations vary by report; verify against primary company announcements before citing a specific number.
