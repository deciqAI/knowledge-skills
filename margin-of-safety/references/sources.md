# Sources — margin-of-safety

> *Primary sources for the [margin-of-safety](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Graham, B., & Dodd, D. L. (1934). *Security Analysis.* McGraw-Hill. ISBN 978-0071592536 (modern edition). The founding text.
- Graham, B. (1949). *The Intelligent Investor.* Harper & Brothers. ISBN 978-0060555665. The accessible synthesis. Chapter 20 specifically on margin of safety.
- Buffett, W. *Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters to Shareholders.* https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html — Buffett's lifelong articulation of the principle in practice.
- Klarman, S. A. (1991). *Margin of Safety: Risk-Averse Value Investing Strategies for the Thoughtful Investor.* HarperBusiness. ISBN 978-0887305108. Modern value-investor application. (Out of print; legendary.)
- Standish, R. K. (Standish Group). *CHAOS Report* (annual). Documents the empirical base rate of software project overruns — 50-200% time and cost overruns are the norm, not exception.
- Lowenstein, R. (1995). *Buffett: The Making of an American Capitalist.* Doubleday. ISBN 978-0385484916. Biographical account of Buffett's American Express investment among others.
