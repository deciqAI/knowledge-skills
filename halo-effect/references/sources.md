# Sources — halo-effect

> *Primary sources for the [halo-effect](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Thorndike, E. L. (1920). "A constant error in psychological ratings." *Journal of Applied Psychology*, 4(1), 25–29. The original observation.
- Nisbett, R. E., & Wilson, T. D. (1977). "The halo effect: Evidence for unconscious alteration of judgments." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, 35(4), 250–256. The formal experiment.
- Rosenzweig, P. (2007). *The Halo Effect: ... and the Eight Other Business Delusions that Deceive Managers.* Free Press. ISBN 978-1416541387. The business critique.
- Asch, S. E. (1946). "Forming impressions of personality." *Journal of Abnormal and Social Psychology*, 41, 258–290. Foundational impression-formation work.
- Schmidt, F. L., & Hunter, J. E. (1998). "The validity and utility of selection methods in personnel psychology." *Psychological Bulletin*, 124, 262–274. Structured vs unstructured interviews.
- Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* Farrar, Straus and Giroux. ISBN 978-0374533557. Chapter 7 on halo.
- Highhouse, S. (2008). "Stubborn reliance on intuition and subjectivity in employee selection." *Industrial and Organizational Psychology*, 1, 333–342.
