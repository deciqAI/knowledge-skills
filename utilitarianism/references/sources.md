# Sources — utilitarianism

> *Primary sources for the [utilitarianism](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **Bentham, Jeremy.** *An Introduction to the Principles of Morals and Legislation.* 1789. **Primary source for the utilitarian foundation.** Verbatim Overview quote from ch. 1. https://archive.org/details/anintroductiont01bentgoog
- **Mill, John Stuart.** *Utilitarianism.* Parker, Son, and Bourn, 1863. **Primary source for the refined utilitarian framework.** Verbatim Overview quote from ch. 2. https://archive.org/details/utilitarianism00mill
- **US Environmental Protection Agency.** *The Benefits and Costs of the Clean Air Act from 1990 to 2020.* EPA-410/R-11-005, April 2011. **Primary source for the Clean Air Act cost-benefit analysis.** Specific figures cited from the Summary Report. https://www.epa.gov/clean-air-act-overview/benefits-and-costs-clean-air-act
- **Sunstein, Cass R.** *The Cost-Benefit Revolution.* MIT Press, 2018. Documents the institutional evolution of cost-benefit analysis as the primary applied utilitarian tool in US regulatory policy, with methodological details.
- **Singer, Peter.** *Practical Ethics,* 3rd ed. Cambridge University Press, 2011. The most rigorous modern treatment of applied utilitarianism, including systematic discussion of the aggregation problem and its limits.

**Not cited and why:** The "trolley problem" (divert a runaway trolley to kill 1 vs. 5) is widely used as a utilitarian illustration in popular presentations — it is not cited here because it is a constructed thought experiment designed to test intuitions, not a real-world utilitarian analysis case. Utilitarian analysis in the real world involves genuine uncertainty, non-binary options, and irreducible information gaps that the trolley problem's stipulated certainty eliminates. The skill's worked example uses a primary-source empirical case instead.
