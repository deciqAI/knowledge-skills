# Method in Action: Nikola Tesla's Three-Radius Divergence, 1880–1943

> *Example for the [three-radius-model](../SKILL.md) skill.*

Nikola Tesla represents one of history's most documented cases of extreme divergence between the three circles — and its consequences.

**Cognition radius — extraordinarily large:** Tesla's understanding spanned AC electrical systems, polyphase motors, radio transmission, resonance phenomena, and early computing concepts. He holds 300+ patents across these domains. His 1919 autobiography documents his ability to run complete engineering simulations in his mind before committing anything to paper: "I do not rush into actual work. When I get an idea, I start at once building it up in my imagination. I change the construction, make improvements and operate the device in my mind."

**Capability radius — excellent in engineering, severely limited in business:** Tesla demonstrated extraordinary engineering capability — he built working prototypes of systems that defined the 20th century. However, he explicitly acknowledged in his autobiography that financial management and business negotiation were not within his capability radius. He gave away the AC motor patents to Westinghouse without negotiating adequate ongoing royalties. He was repeatedly defrauded by business partners because he could not evaluate financial instruments or partnership structures.

**Action radius — collapsed in critical non-technical domains:** Tesla's action radius in later life reflects the Capability-Action gap compounded by the Cognition-Capability gap in business. He understood he needed funding (cognition) but could not evaluate funders (Cognition-Capability gap). He could theoretically have negotiated better terms (arguable capability) but consistently failed to do so (Capability-Action gap). The result: Tesla lived his final years in financial ruin in a hotel, his inventions benefiting others.

**The diagnostic lesson:** Tesla's failure was not a failure of cognition or technical capability. It was a failure to expand the capability radius into the domains that ultimately determined whether his cognition radius would compound value for him or for others — and a failure to close the Capability-Action gap in the business domains where he had some capability but consistently did not act.

**Primary source:** Tesla, N. (1919). *My Inventions: The Autobiography of Nikola Tesla*, originally published in *Electrical Experimenter* magazine, issues February–June and October 1919. Full text: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/13576. Verbatim: "I do not rush into actual work. When I get an idea, I start at once building it up in my imagination.
