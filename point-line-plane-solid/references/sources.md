# Sources — point-line-plane-solid

> *Primary sources for the [point-line-plane-solid](../SKILL.md) skill.*

1. **Kim, W. Chan & Mauborgne, Renée. "Blue Ocean Strategy." *Harvard Business Review*, October 2004.** https://hbr.org/2004/10/blue-ocean-strategy
   > "The strategic logic of value innovation... requires companies to reorient their strategic focus from competitors to alternatives, and from customers to noncustomers of the industry."
   Provides the conceptual underpinning for the move from competing at the point level to redefining the competitive plane — the strategic logic that motivates dimension elevation.

2. **Parker, Geoffrey G., Van Alstyne, Marshall W. & Choudary, Sangeet Paul. *Platform Revolution.* W.W. Norton, 2016.** https://wwnorton.com/books/Platform-Revolution/
   > "Platforms don't just create goods or services; they create communities... When goods are bought and sold, they transfer their value only to the buyer and seller. Network goods are different — a telephone is useless until others have phones."
   The definitive treatment of the line→plane→solid transition mechanics, network effect economics, and platform governance.

3. **Carnegie, Andrew. *Autobiography of Andrew Carnegie* (1920).** https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/17976
   Primary-source documentation of Carnegie's strategic reasoning and the sequential dimension-building approach in his own words.

**What is NOT cited and why:** Michael Porter's Five Forces is omitted — it is a point-level competitive analysis framework and does not model the dimension hierarchy that is this skill's core contribution. Clayton Christensen's disruption theory is relevant to dimension transitions but is omitted because it addresses disruption dynamics rather than dimension architecture.
