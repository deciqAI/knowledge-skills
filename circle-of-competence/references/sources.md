# Sources — circle-of-competence

> *Primary sources for the [circle-of-competence](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Buffett, W. E. (1996). "Letter to Shareholders." *Berkshire Hathaway Annual Report*. https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1996.html. The canonical articulation.
- Buffett, W. E. (1999). "Mr. Buffett on the Stock Market." *Fortune Magazine*, November 22, 1999. The contextual explanation during the tech bubble.
- Munger, C. T. (1994). "A Lesson on Elementary, Worldly Wisdom As It Relates To Investment Management and Business." USC Business School lecture. Reprinted in *Poor Charlie's Almanack* (2005). ISBN 978-1578645015.
- Marks, H. (2011). *The Most Important Thing: Uncommon Sense for the Thoughtful Investor.* Columbia Business School Publishing. ISBN 978-0231153683.
- Buffett, W. E. & Cunningham, L. A. (2013). *The Essays of Warren Buffett: Lessons for Corporate America* (4th ed.). The Cunningham Group. ISBN 978-1611634099.
- Greenwald, B. C. N. et al. (2001). *Value Investing: From Graham to Buffett and Beyond.* Wiley. ISBN 978-0471463399. The academic treatment.
- Pabrai, M. (2007). *The Dhandho Investor.* Wiley. ISBN 978-0470043899. A practitioner's application of the circle discipline.
