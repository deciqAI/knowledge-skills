# Landing Page Conversion — Clarity Beats Cleverness

## Overview
A landing page converts when a visitor, in seconds, understands **what it is, who it's for, why it's better, and what to do next** — with nothing in the way. Most pages leak conversions to vague headlines, buried value, weak proof, and friction. Conversion optimization is mostly removing doubt and steps, not adding persuasion tricks.

## The Process
1. **Nail the above-the-fold answer** — headline states the outcome for the specific buyer; subhead adds the how. *Gate: if a stranger can't say what you do in 5 seconds, nothing below matters.*
2. **Lead with value, not features** — the transformation, then the mechanism.
3. **Add proof near claims** — testimonials, logos, numbers, guarantees placed where doubt arises.
4. **One primary CTA, repeated** — a single next action; remove competing links/choices. *Gate: multiple competing CTAs = decision paralysis, lower conversion.*
5. **Strip friction** — fewer form fields, no forced signup to see value, fast load, mobile-clean.
6. **Test one hypothesis at a time** — headline, offer, CTA; change one variable, measure. *Gate: changing many things at once = you learn nothing.*

## When to Use
- Decent traffic, poor conversion
- New offer/product page launch
- Paid campaigns landing on weak pages (wasting spend)

## Applying It Well
- Message-match the ad/source to the page (same promise).
- Reduce fields and choices before adding copy.
- Watch session recordings/heatmaps to see where attention dies.

## Red Flags
- Clever headline that hides what you sell.
- Feature list with no outcome or proof.
- Many CTAs, long forms, slow mobile load.

## Verification
- [ ] 5-second clarity test passed
- [ ] Value-first message with proof near claims
- [ ] Single repeated primary CTA
- [ ] Friction (fields/steps/load) minimized; one-variable tests
