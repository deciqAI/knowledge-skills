# Sources — pricing-strategy

> *Primary sources for the [pricing-strategy](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **Kahneman, Daniel & Tversky, Amos.** "Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk." *Econometrica*, Vol. 47, No. 2 (March 1979), pp. 263–292. **Primary source for loss aversion and reference points**. JSTOR: https://www.jstor.org/stable/1914185
- **Thaler, Richard.** "Toward a Positive Theory of Consumer Choice." *Journal of Economic Behavior and Organization*, Vol. 1, No. 1 (March 1980), pp. 39–60. **Primary source for endowment effect and mental accounting**. https://doi.org/10.1016/0167-2681(80)90051-7
- **Epstein, Edward Jay.** "Have You Ever Tried to Sell a Diamond?" *The Atlantic Monthly*, February 1982. **Primary-source investigative piece on the De Beers case**, with reproduced N.W. Ayer internal memos. https://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/1982/02/have-you-ever-tried-to-sell-a-diamond/304575/
- **Keating, Gina.** *Netflixed: The Epic Battle for America's Eyeballs*. Portfolio/Penguin, 2012. **Primary source for the 2011 Netflix/Qwikster price-restructuring case**; corroborated by Netflix's Q3 2011 Letter to Shareholders (October 24, 2011).
- **Poundstone, William.** *Priceless: The Myth of Fair Value (and How to Take Advantage of It)*. Hill & Wang, 2010. **Operational synthesis of pricing-psychology experimental literature**.
- **Van Westendorp, Peter H.** "NSS — Price Sensitivity Meter (PSM)." Proceedings of the 29th ESOMAR Congress, 1976. **Standard WTP measurement methodology**.
- The popular framing "people buy benefits, not features" is **not** cited as a source — by this skill's own rule, an aphorism is not evidence. The framework here is operational: identify value in customer units, measure WTP per segment, design anchor + tier structure, frame for loss aversion, measure conversion.
