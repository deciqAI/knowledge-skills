# Sources — hidden-elite-advantages

> *Primary sources for the [hidden-elite-advantages](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary Source 1:**
Merton, R.K. (1968). The Matthew effect in science. *Science*, 159(3810), 56–63. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.159.3810.56

Verbatim: "The Matthew effect consists in the accruing of greater increments of recognition for particular scientific contributions to scientists of considerable repute and the withholding of such recognition from scientists who have not yet made their mark... For it is upon this feature of the reward system that the productivity of the scientific community depends to a considerable degree." (p. 56)

**Primary Source 2:**
Arthur, W.B. (July–August 1996). Increasing returns and the new world of business. *Harvard Business Review*. https://hbr.org/1996/07/increasing-returns-and-the-new-world-of-business

Verbatim: "Companies that gain an edge early on, for whatever reason, find their advantage self-reinforcing... Under increasing returns, the market tilts toward one player: the rich get richer."

**Primary Source 3 (case):**
Browne, J. (1995). *Charles Darwin: Voyaging* (Volume 1). Princeton University Press. (pp. 350–450 on post-Beagle methodology and network development.)

Wallace, A.R. (1858). "On the Tendency of Varieties to Depart Indefinitely from the Original Type." *Journal of the Proceedings of the Linnean Society* (Zoology), 3(9), 53–62. Via Darwin Correspondence Project: https://www.darwinproject.ac.uk/letter/DCP-LETT-2299.xml

**What is NOT cited and why:** Psychological talent research (twin studies, heritability of cognitive traits) is not cited because this skill operates at the level of compound learning and environmental design — the strategic question is what is developable, not the precise heritability coefficient of underlying capacity. The heritability literature would complicate the green/yellow/red classification with statistical nuance that is not actionable at the individual development planning level.
