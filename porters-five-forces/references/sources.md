# Sources — porters-five-forces

> *Primary sources for the [porters-five-forces](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Porter, M. E. (1979). "How Competitive Forces Shape Strategy." *Harvard Business Review*, 57(2), 137-145. The founding paper. Reprinted in the 2008 update: Porter, M. E. (2008). "The Five Competitive Forces That Shape Strategy." *Harvard Business Review*, 86(1), 25-40. https://hbr.org/2008/01/the-five-competitive-forces-that-shape-strategy
- Porter, M. E. (1980). *Competitive Strategy: Techniques for Analyzing Industries and Competitors.* Free Press. The book-length extension. ISBN 978-0684841489.
- McGahan, A. M., & Porter, M. E. (1997). "How Much Does Industry Matter, Really?" *Strategic Management Journal*, 18(S1), 15-30. The empirical variance-decomposition study. https://doi.org/10.1002/(SICI)1097-0266(199707)18:1+<15::AID-SMJ916>3.3.CO;2-T
- Bain, J. S. (1956). *Barriers to New Competition.* Harvard University Press. The IO-economics foundation Porter built on.
- Brandenburger, A. M., & Nalebuff, B. J. (1996). *Co-opetition.* Currency. The complementary "sixth force" extension. ISBN 978-0385479509.
- Damodaran, A. *Industry-level returns dataset*, NYU Stern. http://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/ — updated annually with industry ROIC and other structural metrics.
