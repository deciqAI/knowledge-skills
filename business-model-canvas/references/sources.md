# Sources — business-model-canvas

> *Primary sources for the [business-model-canvas](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **Osterwalder, Alexander & Pigneur, Yves.** *Business Model Generation: A Handbook for Visionaries, Game Changers, and Challengers*. John Wiley & Sons, 2010. **The canonical primary source for the canvas.** The verbatim passage in the Overview is from p. 14.
- **Osterwalder, Alexander.** "The Business Model Ontology — A Proposition in a Design Science Approach." Doctoral dissertation, HEC Lausanne, 2004. **The academic foundation** for the canvas as a design-science ontology. https://serval.unil.ch/resource/serval:BIB_R_4210.P001/REF
- **Freiberg, Kevin & Freiberg, Jackie.** *Nuts! Southwest Airlines' Crazy Recipe for Business and Personal Success*. Bard Press, 1996. Primary-source-rich account of Southwest's founding and culture; multiple Kelleher interviews.
- **Heskett, James L., Sasser, W. Earl, & Wheeler, Joe.** "Southwest Airlines: In a Different World." Harvard Business School Case 9-910-419 (2010) and earlier related HBS cases — standard teaching cases on Southwest's business model.
- **Southwest Airlines Co.,** Annual Reports and SEC 10-K filings (1971–present) — primary-source evidence for the continuous-profitability claim and unit-economics differentiation. https://www.southwestairlinesinvestorrelations.com
- **Gittell, Jody Hoffer.** *The Southwest Airlines Way: Using the Power of Relationships to Achieve High Performance*. McGraw-Hill, 2003. Academic analysis of the operating model.
- **Kashani, Kamran & Miller, Joyce.** "Innovation and Renovation: The Nespresso Story." IMD Case IMD-5-0543, IMD Lausanne, 2000. The standard teaching case on Nespresso's 1986 failure and 1988 razor-and-blades rebuild — primary source for the Nespresso example.
- **Andreessen Horowitz (a16z).** "Navigating the High Cost of AI Compute" (a16z.com, 2023) and a16z's ongoing writing on AI app-layer margins and infrastructure economics — widely-cited industry analysis of inference compute as the dominant variable cost in AI-native business models. Supporting source for the *Mapping an AI-Native Startup (2024–2026)* example.
- **OpenAI API pricing & model-deprecation policy** (platform.openai.com) and **Anthropic API documentation** (docs.anthropic.com) — primary sources for foundation-model providers' published pricing and deprecation practices, illustrating why the model provider sits in both Key Partners and Cost Structure (and carries platform risk) in the AI-native example. Pricing and specific model versions change frequently; consult the live pages for current figures.
- The popular framing "disrupt the industry" is **not** cited here as a source — by this skill's own rule, an aphorism is not evidence. Southwest's differentiation was block-by-block in the canvas, not a generic "disruption."
