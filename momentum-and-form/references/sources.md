# Sources — momentum-and-form

> *Primary sources for the [momentum-and-form](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- *Sun Tzu's Art of War* (《孙子兵法》, *Sunzi Bingfa*), traditionally attributed to Sun Wu (孙武), c. 5th century BCE. Standard modern scholarly text based on the 1972 Yinqueshan (银雀山) Han-dynasty bamboo-slip manuscript discovery. Chapters 4 (《形篇》) and 5 (《势篇》) are the primary source for the form-momentum framework.
- Ames, R. T. (1993). *Sun-Tzu: The Art of Warfare*. Ballantine Books. The most scholarly modern translation, based on the Yinqueshan manuscript. ISBN 978-0345379917.
- Griffith, S. B. (1963). *Sun Tzu: The Art of War*. Oxford University Press. The translation most influential in 20th-century American military doctrine. ISBN 978-0195014761.
- Sawyer, R. D. (1994). *The Art of War*. Westview Press. Detailed scholarly commentary tracing the Chinese commentarial tradition. ISBN 978-0813312293.
- Cao Cao (曹操, 155–220 CE). *Cao Commentary on Sun Tzu's Art of War* (《魏武帝注孙子》). The earliest and most influential historical commentary, by the Han-Wei military commander and statesman.
- Mao Zedong (1937). *On Protracted War* (《论持久战》). Modern Chinese military theorist whose strategic writings draw extensively on Sun Tzu's form-momentum framework as applied to asymmetric warfare. English translation in *Selected Works of Mao Tse-tung*, Volume II.
- U.S. Marine Corps (1989/1997). *Warfighting* (MCDP 1). Doctrinal manual that cites Sun Tzu explicitly and integrates the form-momentum framework into modern American military doctrine.
- Mintzberg, H., Ahlstrand, B., & Lampel, J. (1998). *Strategy Safari: A Guided Tour Through the Wilds of Strategic Management*. Free Press. Engages with the configurational tradition in strategy theory. ISBN 978-0743270571.
- Maples, M. (2024). *Pattern Breakers: Why Some Start-Ups Change the Future*. PublicAffairs. Contemporary venture-investor framework articulating a form-momentum-style analysis under modern terminology ("inflections," "right time"). ISBN 978-1541704350.
