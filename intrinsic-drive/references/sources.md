# Sources — intrinsic-drive

> *Primary sources for the [intrinsic-drive](../SKILL.md) skill.*

1. **Deci, Edward L. & Ryan, Richard M. *Intrinsic Motivation and Self-Determination in Human Behavior.* Plenum Press, 1985.** https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4899-2271-7
   > "When people are intrinsically motivated, they experience interest and enjoyment, they feel competent and self-determining, and in a very real sense, their behavior reflects authentic functioning in the fullest sense."
   The foundational empirical treatment of intrinsic motivation, self-determination theory, and the documented crowding-out effects of external rewards on intrinsic sources.

2. **Csikszentmihalyi, Mihaly. *Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience.* Harper & Row, 1990.** https://www.harpercollins.com/products/flow-mihaly-csikszentmihalyi
   > "The best moments usually occur when a person's body or mind is stretched to its limits in a voluntary effort to accomplish something difficult and worthwhile... Such experiences are not necessarily pleasant at the time, but in the long run... provide a deep sense of enjoyment."
   Documents Cluster 3 and 4 drive sources and the conditions under which intrinsic engagement reaches its maximum (flow state).

3. **Curie, Eve. *Madame Curie: A Biography.* (1937), translated by Vincent Sheean.** https://archive.org/details/madamecurie012961mbp
   Primary-source biographical documentation of Marie Curie's multi-cluster drive architecture over 43 years.

**What is NOT cited and why:** Daniel Pink's *Drive* (2010) is omitted — it is a popular synthesis of Deci/Ryan without primary research contribution; citing it alongside the primary source would imply equal status. Carol Dweck's *Mindset* (2006) is relevant but addresses the belief layer (neurological levels Cluster 4 belief: "I can grow") rather than the motivation source portfolio — it is a related but distinct construct.
