# Method in Action: Marie Curie's Motivational Architecture (1891–1934)

> *Example for the [intrinsic-drive](../SKILL.md) skill.*

Marie Curie's sustained drive over 43 years of scientific work — through conditions that would have defeated most — provides the clearest documented example of a multi-cluster intrinsic drive portfolio.

**Cluster 1 (Life Force):** Curie's letters to her sister Bronya from Paris (1891) document a passion for physics that predated any external recognition or goal: "Everything is a wonder to me, and I am always finding new subjects of admiration." Her grit was observable in her doctoral defense (1903, the first woman to earn a PhD in France) and her return to research after the death of her husband Pierre (1906), an event that would have ended most scientific careers.

**Cluster 2 (Goals):** A visible drive to prove that women could achieve at the highest levels of science. This was active throughout the early career and partially satisfied by the first Nobel Prize (Physics, 1903). The partial exhaustion of this cluster did not end her drive — it shifted its center of gravity.

**Cluster 3 (Cognition):** Her laboratory notebooks at the Bibliothèque nationale de France document insatiable curiosity about radioactive phenomena — curiosity that generated new research questions faster than she could answer existing ones. These notebooks (still radioactive, stored in lead-lined boxes) show the ongoing engagement of a mind that genuinely needed to know.

**Cluster 4 (Beyond Self):** Most durable. Curie's deployment of mobile X-ray units ("petites Curies") during World War I — her personal invention — connected her research directly to the mission of saving wounded soldiers' lives. She stated: "I am resolved to put all my strength at the service of my country." This mission source sustained her through the most difficult period of her life (1914–1918) and carried her through to the founding of the Curie Institute (1920) and her second Nobel Prize (Chemistry, 1911, awarded during the worst period of public hostility following her relationship with Paul Langevin).

The portfolio architecture is visible: when Cluster 2 partially depleted, Clusters 3 and 4 sustained forward motion. When Cluster 3 was constrained by war, Cluster 4 (mission) dominated. No single cluster failure collapsed the whole.
