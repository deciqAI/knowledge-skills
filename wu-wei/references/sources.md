# Sources — wu-wei

> *Primary sources for the [wu-wei](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Laozi (老子). *Daodejing* (道德经, c. 4th century BCE). Translations: D.C. Lau (Penguin, 1963), ISBN 978-0140441314; Roger Ames & David Hall, *Dao De Jing: A Philosophical Translation* (Ballantine, 2003), ISBN 978-0345434104; Stephen Mitchell (HarperPerennial, 1992), ISBN 978-0061142666 (accessible but loose).
- Zhuangzi (莊子). *Zhuangzi* (c. 3rd century BCE). Translations: Burton Watson, *The Complete Works of Chuang Tzu* (Columbia, 1968/2013), ISBN 978-0231164740; Brook Ziporyn, *Zhuangzi: The Complete Writings* (Hackett, 2020), ISBN 978-1624668555.
- Csikszentmihalyi, M. (1990). *Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience.* Harper & Row. ISBN 978-0061339202. The empirical research that re-derived wu wei in psychological terms.
- Slingerland, E. (2014). *Trying Not to Try: The Art and Science of Spontaneity.* Crown. ISBN 978-0770437619. The most rigorous modern synthesis of Daoist wu wei and contemporary cognitive science.
- Ames, R. T. (1998). *Wandering at Ease in the Zhuangzi.* SUNY Press. ISBN 978-0791439210. A scholarly collection on Daoist philosophy of action.
- Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* Farrar, Straus and Giroux. ISBN 978-0374275631. The System 1 / System 2 framework that maps neatly onto wu wei / wei.
- Lao Tzu (Tao Te Ching) chapters 2, 17, 48, 63 — the most directly wu-wei-related chapters.
