# Sources — self-evolution-3d

> *Primary sources for the [self-evolution-3d](../SKILL.md) skill.*

## Sources

**Primary sources with verbatim quotes:**

1. Kegan, R. *The Evolving Self: Problem and Process in Human Development*. Harvard University Press, 1982. https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674272316
   > "The evolution of the self is not the accumulation of experience but the transformation of the meaning-making system itself. Each new order of consciousness does not add to what the person already is; it transforms it."

2. Dweck, C.S. *Mindset: The New Psychology of Success*. Random House, 2006. https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/44330/mindset-by-carol-s-dweck-phd/
   > "In a fixed mindset, people believe their basic qualities, like their intelligence or talent, are simply fixed traits... In a growth mindset, people believe that their most basic abilities can be developed through dedication and hard work."

3. Nightingale, F. *Notes on Nursing: What It Is, and What It Is Not*. Harrison, 1860. https://digital.library.upenn.edu/women/nightingale/nursing/nursing.html
   > "The very first requirement in a hospital is that it should do the sick no harm... It is the most useful and interesting of occupations, but it needs to be approached with a clear head, not a sentimental one."

**What is NOT cited and why:**

- Maslow, A., "A Theory of Human Motivation" (1943) describes a hierarchy of needs that superficially resembles a Height axis — but Maslow's hierarchy is motivational and need-based, not developmental and dimensional. Self-actualization in Maslow is a ceiling; Height in the 3D framework is an axis with no inherent ceiling. Including Maslow would conflate motivational theory with developmental theory.
- Goleman, D., *Emotional Intelligence* (1995) is relevant to the Depth dimension but describes a trait model rather than a developmental practice model. The 3D framework's Depth axis is developmental — it grows through structured practice — not a stable trait that some people have and others don't. Citing Goleman would risk importing the fixed-mindset framing that Dweck's research explicitly corrects.
