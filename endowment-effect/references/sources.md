# Sources — endowment-effect

> *Primary sources for the [endowment-effect](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Thaler, R. (1980). "Toward a Positive Theory of Consumer Choice." *Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization*, 1(1), 39–60. Coined the term; theoretical foundation.
- Kahneman, D., Knetsch, J. L., & Thaler, R. H. (1990). "Experimental Tests of the Endowment Effect and the Coase Theorem." *Journal of Political Economy*, 98(6), 1325–1348. The Cornell mug experiment; definitive empirical proof.
- Kahneman, D., Knetsch, J. L., & Thaler, R. H. (1991). "Anomalies: The Endowment Effect, Loss Aversion, and Status Quo Bias." *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, 5(1), 193–206. The unifying trio paper placing endowment in the loss-aversion / status-quo cluster.
- Kahneman, D., & Tversky, A. (1979). "Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk." *Econometrica*, 47(2), 263–291. The mechanistic foundation: loss aversion explains the endowment premium.
- Thaler, R. H. (2015). *Misbehaving: The Making of Behavioral Economics.* W. W. Norton. ISBN 978-0393080940. Thaler's first-person account of discovering and naming the endowment effect.
- Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* Farrar, Straus and Giroux. ISBN 978-0374533557. Chapter 27 integrates endowment, loss aversion, and status quo bias for a general audience.
- Morewedge, C. K., & Giblin, C. E. (2015). "Explanations of the Endowment Effect: An Integrative Review." *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 19(6), 339–348. Modern synthesis of mechanisms.
