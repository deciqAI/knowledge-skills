# Founder Mindset

## Overview

A specific cognitive mode for running an early-stage company — different from manager mindset (optimizing known systems) and employee mindset (executing within fixed scope). Founders search for the system itself under conditions of extreme uncertainty, asymmetric risk, and personal execution bottlenecks. The two load-bearing disciplines: **Default Alive or Default Dead** (Graham 2015) and **doing things that don't scale** (Graham 2013).

**Compose:** [first-principles](../first-principles/SKILL.md) on inherited assumptions about how companies operate · [regret-minimization](../regret-minimization/SKILL.md) for the decision to start or quit · [inversion](../inversion/SKILL.md) to surface failure paths founders underweight · [lean-startup](../lean-startup/SKILL.md) + [mvp](../mvp/SKILL.md) operationally · [metacognition](../metacognition/SKILL.md) to catch monitoring lapses under founder stress.

## When to Use

Apply when: running an early-stage company (hire/fire/pivot/raise/cut burn decisions) · comfort-seeking is creeping in · cannot tell if on path to success or slow death · team is scaling prematurely · a specific hard problem has been on the list > 2 weeks · deciding how much to raise/burn/hire amid the AI-startup gold rush (abundant capital, AI bubble talk, tiny high-agency teams) · someone says *"founder mindset," "founder mode," "default alive or default dead," "do things that don't scale," "am I avoiding the hard thing?"*

**When NOT to use:** mature well-understood business · employee within a startup · purely tactical decision · "founder mindset" as performative identity.

## The Process

Run the **Founder-Mode Audit**: Default Alive math → avoiding-the-hard-thing → don't-scale-yet → personal-stress check.

1. **Compute Default Alive vs Default Dead.** Take current revenue growth, expense growth, and bank cash. Will the company reach profitability before cash runs out without raising? If yes → Default Alive. If no → Default Dead. Re-run quarterly minimum. Founders systematically misclassify themselves as Default Alive when they are Default Dead.
2. **Name the hard thing you are avoiding.** What specific painful action has been on your list for > 2 weeks? Difficult customer call, needed firing, co-founder misalignment conversation. Write it down. Schedule the action within 7 days.
3. **Check what you are scaling prematurely.** Hiring, automation, infrastructure, or paid acquisition that requires something underneath to be proven first but isn't yet. Common: VP of Sales before founder has closed 20+ deals; paid acquisition before organic conversion is solid.
4. **Check what you are not doing that doesn't scale.** Who would you talk to personally? Which customer would you visit physically? The unscalable thing the founder is *not* doing is often more diagnostic than the strategy on the slide deck.
5. **Personal stress / pattern check.** Avoidance · delegation to nobody-in-particular · excessive meeting attendance · comfort-seeking on optimization tasks. Name which pattern is present. Naming is the first move.
6. **Founder-mode action selected.** From steps 2–5, pick ONE action for the next 7 days you have been avoiding. Not three; one.
7. **Re-run weekly until shipped.** Schedule for every Monday — without the ritual, the avoidance pattern reasserts within ~10 days.

## Founder Packs

Founder mindset operates the same across industries; the specific hard things differ by company type.

- **B2B SaaS:** unscalable = founder-led sales for first ~$1M ARR; premature = VP of Sales before 20+ personal closes.
- **Consumer app:** unscalable = founder physically watching users; premature = paid acquisition before organic retention is solid.
- **Marketplace:** unscalable = manually matching supply/demand in first city; premature = multiple geographic launches simultaneously.
- **Deep-tech / hardware:** unscalable = founder personally handling first customer integrations; premature = mass manufacturing before first 100 units perform reliably.

## Applying It Well

- **Run Default Alive monthly.** Founders misclassify ~3 months earlier when running monthly vs quarterly.
- **The avoided thing IS the signal.** Deferring > 2 weeks almost certainly means that action is the founder-mode move.
- **Founder mode is uncomfortable on purpose.** If the audit feels good, it isn't working.
- **Unscalable is a bridge, not a destination.** Automate once the pattern is proven.

## Red Flags

- Default Alive / Default Dead has not been computed in the last 60 days
- A specific painful action has been on the list for > 2 weeks
- Founder calendar shows < 10% customer-facing time
- A VP-level hire was made before the founder personally proved that function's work
- Internal-process improvement projects are getting more attention than customer-facing work
- The same hard problem keeps appearing in weekly priorities but never moves
- "Founder mode" is being used as identity rather than checked as operational discipline
