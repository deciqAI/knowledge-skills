# Method in Action: Netflix's 2010–2013 Strategic Pivot

> *Example for the [swot-analysis](../SKILL.md) skill.*

Netflix's transition from DVD-by-mail to streaming — one of the most successful deliberate pivots in entertainment history — can be reconstructed from public filings as a SWOT-driven analysis:

**Step 1 — Unit and purpose:** Netflix Inc. as of mid-2010; decision: whether and how fast to transition the core business model to streaming.

**Step 2 — Strengths:** (S1) ~20 million subscriber base with high brand trust; (S2) deep content licensing library; (S3) a recommendation algorithm (Cinematch) proprietary to Netflix; (S4) positive free cash flow (~$160M net income in 2010) to fund transition.

**Step 3 — Weaknesses:** (W1) core DVD economics dependent on physical media — high per-unit cost (shipping, inventory, reverse logistics); (W2) streaming content library thin relative to DVD library; (W3) limited negotiating power with content studios compared to established cable networks.

**Step 4 — Opportunities:** (O1) US home broadband penetration rose from 42% (2006) to 70% (2010); (O2) the iPad launched in April 2010, creating a new "lean-back" screen category; (O3) cable subscription pricing rising, creating buyer willingness to seek alternatives.

**Step 5 — Threats:** (T1) Hulu, backed by NBC Universal and Fox, gaining premium content licensing; (T2) Amazon entering content distribution with Prime; (T3) content studios gaining leverage to raise licensing fees as streaming demand grew.

**Step 6 — TOWS:** The critical SO strategy was S1×O1 — use the existing subscriber base and cash flow to finance the streaming build-out, capturing the broadband penetration wave before cable companies responded. The ST strategy was S4×T3 — use cash reserves to pre-license content at still-reasonable rates before studios understood their leverage. The WO strategy was W3×O3 — offer studios subscriber data in exchange for licensing deals, creating a negotiating lever they lacked with traditional distributors.

**Step 7:** Netflix explicitly de-prioritized WT (defensive protection of the DVD business) by separating it into Qwikster in 2011 — a decision that faced consumer backlash but was strategically coherent as a way to force organizational attention onto streaming.

**Validation:** Netflix market capitalization grew from approximately $9B (2010) to approximately $150B (2020) on the back of this strategic pivot. The SWOT reconstructed above maps cleanly to the company's actual strategic execution per Reed Hastings' interviews and investor letters from the period.

Primary source: Netflix annual reports 2010–2013; Hastings, R. (2011). Shareholder letters on Netflix website. US broadband penetration: Pew Research Center Internet Usage surveys.
