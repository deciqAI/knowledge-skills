# Method in Action: ABB Global Management Meetings (1990s)

> *Example for the [six-thinking-hats](../SKILL.md) skill.*

A primary-source-documented case. Percy Barnevik, CEO of ABB (the Swedish-Swiss engineering conglomerate) from 1988 to 1996, documented the adoption of the Six Hats method in ABB's management structure in interviews and in de Bono's subsequent documentation of the case.

ABB's management team represented 12+ nationalities with structurally different meeting cultures. Northern European (Swedish, Swiss, Finnish) managers operated on consensus norms — exhaustive discussion before any conclusion. American and British managers operated on adversarial norms — position-staking and rebuttal. When these cultures met in a common management session, the result was a collision: consensus-culture managers experienced American directness as aggression; adversarial-culture managers experienced Scandinavian consensus-seeking as evasion. Standard strategic meetings were running 10–12 hours without clear conclusions.

Barnevik adopted Six Thinking Hats as a cross-cultural meeting protocol — not as a "creativity tool" but as a **shared language for thinking modes** that made cultural differences irrelevant. Under the hat structure:

- **Blue Hat opening (Barnevik):** "We are evaluating whether to close the Mannheim plant. We will use White → Black → Yellow → Green → Blue in 45 minutes total."
- **White Hat (all):** Plant output data, workforce numbers, union contract terms, logistics cost differentials. Gaps: forward demand projections beyond 18 months were soft.
- **Black Hat (all):** Union agreement triggers, community impact creating regulatory risk, loss of specialized tooling knowledge.
- **Yellow Hat (all):** Cost savings if consolidated, freed capital for eastern European expansion.
- **Green Hat (all):** Partial automation alternative, joint venture with local operator, phased reduction over 3 years.
- **Blue Hat close:** Decision deferred 60 days pending hard demand data; Green Hat phased-reduction option assigned for costing.

Barnevik reported that comparable decisions that had previously taken 12-hour sessions were resolving in under 2 hours. De Bono documented the ABB adoption in *Six Thinking Hats* revised editions and in his later work *Parallel Thinking* (Viking, 1994). The mechanism: hat structure converted cultural conflict into structured sequence, eliminating the mutual-incomprehension dynamic.

**Source:** de Bono, Edward. *Six Thinking Hats*, revised edition (Back Bay Books/Little, Brown, 1999), including corporate adoption section. Also: de Bono, Edward. *Parallel Thinking: From Socratic to de Bono Thinking.* Viking, 1994.
