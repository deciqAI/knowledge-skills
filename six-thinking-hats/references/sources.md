# Sources — six-thinking-hats

> *Primary sources for the [six-thinking-hats](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- **de Bono, Edward.** *Six Thinking Hats.* Little, Brown & Company, 1985. **Primary source defining the six hats and the parallel thinking framework.** Verbatim Overview quotes from ch. 1 and ch. 2.
- **de Bono, Edward.** *Six Thinking Hats,* revised edition. Back Bay Books/Little, Brown, 1999. **Includes ABB and IBM corporate adoption documentation.**
- **de Bono, Edward.** *Parallel Thinking: From Socratic to de Bono Thinking.* Viking, 1994. **Develops the theoretical foundation for parallel vs. adversarial thinking** with additional corporate case studies.
- **Kelley, Tom.** *The Art of Innovation.* Doubleday, 2001. Independent documentation of parallel thinking methods in product development contexts, corroborating de Bono's corporate adoption claims.
- **Schwarz, Roger.** *The Skilled Facilitator,* 3rd ed. Jossey-Bass, 2017. https://www.josseybass.com/en-us/books/the-skilled-facilitator-3rd-edition/  Provides empirical grounding for facilitation norms that align with the Six Hats meeting structure.

### Contemporary sources (2024–2026 example)

- **Anthropic.** *Introducing the Model Context Protocol* (2024). https://www.anthropic.com/news/model-context-protocol — Primary announcement of MCP as an open standard for connecting models to tools and data; grounds the White/Green Hat "integration depth" points in the AI-agents example.
- **McKinsey & Company.** *The State of AI* (annual global survey, 2025 edition). https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai — Widely-cited survey documenting rising enterprise AI adoption and the persistent pilot-to-production gap; grounds the White/Black Hat adoption and deployment-gap claims.

**Not cited and why:** Popular claims that "ABB reduced meeting time from 12 to 2 hours" are cited from de Bono's own documentation of Barnevik's account — there is no independent third-party audit of this specific figure, and it is presented here as an illustrative claim from a primary participant, not as independently verified data. Fortune 500 adoption statistics cited in some secondary sources lack primary survey methodology documentation and are not used as evidence here.
