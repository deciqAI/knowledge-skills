# Sources — customer-relationship-ladder

> *Primary sources for the [customer-relationship-ladder](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary Source 1:**
Lou Gerstner, *Who Says Elephants Can't Dance? Inside IBM's Historic Turnaround* (2002, HarperBusiness). ISBN: 0-06-052390-2.
> "Our customers didn't want another hardware vendor. They wanted someone who could help them transform their businesses... I had to convince IBM people that we weren't in the hardware business anymore."
> — p. 128

IBM Annual Reports 1992–2001, documenting Services revenue growth from $7.4B to $33.2B:
https://www.ibm.com/annualreport/

**Primary Source 2:**
Philip Kotler, Neil Rackham, and Suj Krishnaswamy, "Ending the War Between Sales and Marketing," *Harvard Business Review*, July–August 2006. https://hbr.org/2006/07/ending-the-war-between-sales-and-marketing
> "The most advanced customer relationships are characterized not by frequency of contact but by the depth of the customer's willingness to share internal information and involve the vendor in their planning process."

**What is NOT cited and why:** The 5-rung ladder structure and the 3D-T Framework operationalization are synthesized constructs built for this skill — they do not derive from a single citable source but integrate relationship marketing research (Berry 1983, Dwyer et al. 1987) with account management practice. The IBM case is drawn entirely from primary documentation.
