# Sources — premortem

> *Primary sources for the [premortem](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Klein, G. (2007). "Performing a project premortem." *Harvard Business Review*, 85(9), 18-19. The mainstream introduction.
- Mitchell, D. J., Russo, J. E., & Pennington, N. (1989). "Back to the future: Temporal perspective in the explanation of events." *Journal of Behavioral Decision Making*, 2(1), 25-38. The empirical foundation for prospective hindsight.
- Klein, G. (2011). *Streetlights and Shadows: Searching for the Keys to Adaptive Decision Making.* MIT Press. ISBN 978-0262516723.
- Veinott, B., Klein, G., & Wiggins, S. (2010). "Evaluating the effectiveness of the premortem technique on plan confidence." *7th International ISCRAM Conference Proceedings*.
- Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* Farrar, Straus and Giroux. ISBN 978-0374275631. Chapter 24, the explicit endorsement.
- Russo, J. E., & Schoemaker, P. J. H. (1989). *Decision Traps: The Ten Barriers to Brilliant Decision-Making.* Doubleday. ISBN 978-0385248358.
- Heuer, R. J. Jr., & Pherson, R. H. (2010). *Structured Analytic Techniques for Intelligence Analysis.* CQ Press. ISBN 978-1608710188.
- Sunstein, C. R., & Hastie, R. (2014). *Wiser: Getting Beyond Groupthink to Make Groups Smarter.* Harvard Business Review Press. ISBN 978-1422122990.
- Graham, P. "Default Alive or Default Dead?" (2015, paulgraham.com). The runway/funding-regime framing used in the 2024–2026 AI-launch example — force the "does this survive without the next round?" question early. Widely used in Y Combinator practice.
- a16z (Andreessen Horowitz), writing on the AI application layer and "is it a feature or a company?" (2023–2025, a16z.com); and contemporaneous AI-market analysis in The Information and Stratechery (Ben Thompson) on AI capital expenditure, falling per-token inference prices, model-release cadence, and AI-startup unit economics/valuations. Background for the 2024–2026 example; treat all figures as directional and as of early 2026.
- Stratechery / Ben Thompson (thompson, various 2023–2025, stratechery.com) on foundation-model commoditization and where durable value accrues in the AI stack. Background for the "commoditized by the next release" and moat-in-the-wrong-layer points in the 2024–2026 agent-startup back-casting example; directional, as of early 2026.
- Contemporaneous 2024–2026 reporting and analysis on enterprise adoption of autonomous AI *agents* — the enlarged safety/trust blast radius when a system takes actions rather than only suggests, and the token-cost profile of multi-step agent workflows (e.g., The Information, a16z.com). Background for the safety-incident and unit-economics clusters in the agent-startup example; treat all figures as directional and as of early 2026.
