# Sources — framing-effect

> *Primary sources for the [framing-effect](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Druckman, J. N. (2004). "Political preference formation: Competition, deliberation, and the (ir)relevance of framing effects." *American Political Science Review*, 98(4), 671-686.
- Gallagher, K. M., & Updegraff, J. A. (2012). "Health message framing effects on attitudes, intentions, and behavior: A meta-analytic review." *Annals of Behavioral Medicine*, 43(1), 101-116.
- Thaler, R. H., & Sunstein, C. R. (2008). *Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth, and Happiness.* Yale University Press. ISBN 978-0300122237.
- Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* Farrar, Straus and Giroux. ISBN 978-0374275631. Chapters 33-34 on framing and reversals.
- Luntz, F. (2007). *Words That Work: It's Not What You Say, It's What People Hear.* Hachette. ISBN 978-1401302597.
