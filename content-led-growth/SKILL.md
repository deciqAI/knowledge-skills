# Content-Led Growth — Topic Clusters for Search Intent

## Overview
Content-led growth turns a website into a **compounding acquisition asset** by covering the topics your buyers search, organized as **pillar + cluster** pages that build topical authority. It's slow to start and durable once it works — the opposite of paid, which stops when spend stops. The unit of strategy is **search intent**, not keywords in isolation.

## The Process
1. **Map buyer questions by intent** — informational (learn), commercial (compare), transactional (buy). Mine real questions (sales calls, communities, search).
2. **Choose pillar topics** you can credibly own (tied to your positioning and best-fit audience).
3. **Build clusters** — a pillar page + supporting articles answering the sub-questions, interlinked. *Gate: publishing scattered posts with no cluster/interlink structure = no topical authority.*
4. **Match format to intent** — comparison/ROI pages convert; how-to pages attract; don't force a buy CTA onto a learn query.
5. **Earn authority** — depth, primary sources, and links beat volume of thin posts.
6. **Measure the right lag** — rankings/traffic move over months; track leading signals (indexing, impressions) early. *Gate: judging content by week-1 conversions kills the channel prematurely.*

## When to Use
- Need for durable, non-paid acquisition
- Buyers who research before buying
- SMBs/creators without large ad budgets

## Applying It Well
- Bottom-of-funnel commercial pages first (fastest revenue), then widen.
- One strong cluster beats fifty orphan posts.
- Repurpose one pillar into social/email/video (feeds a content-calendar agent).

## Red Flags
- Keyword stuffing without intent match.
- Orphan posts, no pillar/cluster structure.
- Abandoning the channel before the multi-month payoff.

## Verification
- [ ] Topics mapped to search intent
- [ ] Pillar + interlinked clusters, not orphan posts
- [ ] Format matched to intent
- [ ] Leading indicators tracked over a realistic horizon
