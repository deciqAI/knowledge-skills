# Sources — self-renewal

> *Primary sources for the [self-renewal](../SKILL.md) skill.*

**Primary sources with verbatim quotes:**

1. Dewey, J. *How We Think* (1910). D.C. Heath & Co., Chapter 2. https://archive.org/details/howwethink00dewe
   > "The most important attitude that can be formed is that of desire to go on learning. If impetus in this direction is weakened instead of being intensified, something has gone wrong with our schooling."

2. Dweck, C.S. *Mindset: The New Psychology of Success* (2006). Random House. https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/44330/mindset-by-carol-s-dweck-phd/
   > "In a growth mindset, people believe that their most basic abilities can be developed through dedication and hard work—brains and talent are just the starting point."
   
   Supporting research: Dweck, C.S. & Leggett, E.L. (1988). "A social-cognitive approach to motivation and personality." *Psychological Review*, 95(2), 256-273. https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295X.95.2.256

3. Darwin, C. *The Autobiography of Charles Darwin 1809-1882* (1887, ed. Francis Darwin). https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/2010
   > "I worked on true Baconian principles, and without any theory collected facts on a wholesale scale."

**What is NOT cited and why:**

The 10-obstacle framework (知识结构过时, 观念老化, etc.) and the "否定自己" (self-denial) practice are drawn from Chinese management and leadership literature on organizational renewal. These concepts are widely used in practitioner contexts but do not have a single canonical primary source attributable to a specific author and publication; they represent a synthesis of practice wisdom. Users seeking academic grounding for these specific constructs should consult: Argyris, C. & Schön, D.A. *Organizational Learning* (1978) for the double-loop learning concept that most closely parallels "否定自己" in the Western research tradition.
