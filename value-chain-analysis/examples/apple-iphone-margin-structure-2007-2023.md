# Method in Action: Apple's Value Chain and the iPhone Margin Structure (2007–2023)

> *Example for the [value-chain-analysis](../SKILL.md) skill.*

Apple's iPhone is one of the most analyzed value chains in business history, with sufficient public data to test the framework's claims against observable outcomes.

**Step 1 — Activity map:** Apple's iPhone value chain primary activities include: Inbound Logistics (component sourcing — TSMC for chips, Samsung/LG for OLED screens, Foxconn coordination); Operations (final assembly subcontracted to Foxconn — Apple designs but does not manufacture); Outbound Logistics (Apple Store and authorized reseller distribution); Marketing and Sales (Apple Store retail experience and brand communications); Service (AppleCare, Genius Bar).

Support activities: Technology Development (internal chip design — A-series, M-series — and iOS/macOS software); HR Management (high-density engineering talent; ~2024 salary data from Glassdoor shows Apple engineering comp 30–50% above median tech); Procurement (long-term supply agreements with strategic suppliers; pre-payment arrangements securing priority component access).

**Step 2 — Cost allocation:** iPhone BOM (bill of materials) has been estimated by research firms (iFixit, Counterpoint Research) at approximately $250–$300 for a $999 iPhone model, with the chip package (TSMC manufactured A-series) representing approximately $40–$50 of that cost. Apple's gross margin on iPhone approximates 38–42% (Apple 10-K filings). Operations cost (Foxconn assembly fees) represents approximately $8–$12 per device.

**Step 3 — Differentiation sources:** Two activities are genuine differentiation sources:
- *Technology Development (chip design):* Apple's A-series and M-series chips consistently outperform Qualcomm and Samsung equivalents on performance-per-watt benchmarks. This chip advantage enables features (computational photography, on-device ML) that cannot be replicated on Android hardware without the same silicon investment. The differentiation is durable because chip design requires 3–5 year design cycles and proprietary IP.
- *Marketing and Sales (Apple Store retail):* Apple Store's revenue per square foot (approximately $6,700 in 2023) exceeds nearly any luxury retail benchmark. The retail experience functions as a brand amplifier — converting product quality into premium price realization. This advantage is durable because it requires thousands of trained staff, a global real estate portfolio, and 25+ years of brand investment to replicate.

**Step 4 — Competitor comparison:** Samsung manufactures its own chips (Exynos) and screens (OLED), achieving vertical integration that Apple lacks. Samsung's advantage: component cost below market. Apple's advantage: chip design quality above Samsung's Exynos performance and iOS software ecosystem lock-in. Samsung's retail costs (wholesale model relying on carriers and third-party retail) are structurally lower but sacrifice brand experience.

**Step 5 — Key linkage:** The critical linkage is Technology Development (chip design) → Operations (Foxconn manufacturing at Apple's spec) → Service (software updates over 5–7 years). Apple's chip design enables iOS longevity, which drives device replacement cycles that are longer than Android average — reducing upgrade incentive but building brand loyalty and Services revenue.

**Step 6 — Strategic implication:** Apple's value chain strategy: outsource manufacturing (comparatively low differentiation, high capital intensity); internalize chip design and software (highest differentiation, hardest to replicate); invest in retail experience (brand amplifier capturing differentiation in pricing). The result: Apple sells at approximately 4× the margin of its closest hardware competitor while assembling products on the same Foxconn production lines.

Primary sources: Apple 10-K filings (2007–2023), available at https://investor.apple.com/sec-filings/annual-reports/default.aspx. BOM estimates from Counterpoint Research and iFixit teardown analyses (2022–2023).
