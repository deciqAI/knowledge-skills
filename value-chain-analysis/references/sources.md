# Sources — value-chain-analysis

> *Primary sources for the [value-chain-analysis](../SKILL.md) skill.*

- Porter, M.E. (1985). *Competitive Advantage: Creating and Sustaining Superior Performance.* Free Press. Chapter 2, "The Value Chain and Competitive Advantage," pp. 33–61. Primary source for the framework's primary/support activity structure and disposition analysis. ISBN 978-0684841465. Available in academic libraries.
- Porter, M.E. (1980). *Competitive Strategy.* Free Press. The companion volume on industry structure (Five Forces). ISBN 978-0684841489.
- Shank, J.K., & Govindarajan, V. (1992). "Strategic Cost Management and the Value Chain." *Journal of Cost Management*, Winter 1992. The primary extension of Porter's value chain into management accounting practice. Introduced activity-level cost driver analysis as the operational implementation of Porter's framework.
- Apple Inc. Annual Reports (Form 10-K), 2007–2023. Primary source for the worked example cost and margin data. https://investor.apple.com/sec-filings/annual-reports/default.aspx
- Dediu, H. (2012–2023). Asymco analyses of Apple's value chain and margin structure. Horace Dediu's data-driven commentary is the most detailed public analysis of Apple's activity economics. https://www.asymco.com
- Ghemawat, P., & Nueno, J.L. (2003). "ZARA: Fast Fashion." Harvard Business School Case 703-497. Boston: Harvard Business School Publishing. Primary source for the Zara worked example — documents Inditex's vertically integrated production, Arteixo distribution, lead times, markdown rates, and advertising spend versus fast-fashion rivals.

- Nvidia Corporation. Annual and quarterly SEC filings (Form 10-K FY2024 and FY2025; Form 10-Q reports), 2024–2025. Primary source for the AI-stack worked example — data-center revenue growth and gross-margin data. https://investor.nvidia.com
- TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company). Investor relations, quarterly results, and annual reports, 2024–2025. Primary source for leading-edge fabrication and CoWoS advanced-packaging capacity, and foundry gross-margin data in the AI-stack example. https://investor.tsmc.com
- Microsoft, Amazon, and Alphabet quarterly earnings disclosures, 2024–2025. Primary source for hyperscaler AI capital-expenditure figures cited in the AI-stack example (company investor-relations sites and SEC filings).

Not cited and why: popular framings of "Apple's secret" as purely brand or design culture are not primary-source documented at the activity-cost level and are not used as evidence here. BCG experience curve analyses are often cited alongside value chain without noting that they apply specifically to the Operations activity — this conflation is avoided.
