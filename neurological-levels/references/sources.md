# Sources — neurological-levels

> *Primary sources for the [neurological-levels](../SKILL.md) skill.*

1. **Bateson, Gregory. *Steps to an Ecology of Mind.* University of Chicago Press, 1972.** https://archive.org/details/stepstoecology00bate
   > "There is a hierarchy of types of learning... Learning I is change in specificity of response by correction of errors of choice within a set of alternatives. Learning II is change in the process of Learning I... Learning III is change in the process of Learning II."
   The foundational hierarchical learning framework that established the asymmetry of change across levels — the conceptual bedrock on which Dilts built the Neurological Levels model.

2. **Dilts, Robert. *Changing Belief Systems with NLP.* Meta Publications, 1990.** https://www.nlpu.com/Articles/LevelsSummary.htm
   > "Neurological levels create a natural hierarchy of classification in experience... Changes at higher levels will necessarily create change at lower levels. Changes at lower levels may, but will not necessarily, affect higher levels."
   The direct source of the Neurological Levels model and its diagnostic application to belief and identity change.

3. **Lincoln, A. (1863). *Emancipation Proclamation.* National Archives.** https://www.archives.gov/exhibits/featured-documents/emancipation-proclamation
   Primary-source documentation of the Level 5–6 intervention and its cascade effects. National Archives contextual note: "The Emancipation Proclamation... changed the character of the Civil War."

**What is NOT cited and why:** Tony Robbins' popular "levels of change" framework is omitted — it is derived from Dilts without the Bateson theoretical grounding and conflates levels with different concepts. Organizational culture frameworks (Schein, Kotter) are not cited because they describe culture layers but do not provide the diagnostic asymmetry model that is this skill's core contribution.
