# Sources — structured-communication

> *Primary sources for the [structured-communication](../SKILL.md) skill.*

## Sources

**Primary:**

Aristotle. *Rhetoric.* c. 350 BC. Translated by W. Rhys Roberts. MIT Internet Classics Archive.
URL: https://classics.mit.edu/Aristotle/rhetoric.1.i.html
Verbatim: "Of the modes of persuasion furnished by the spoken word there are three kinds. The first kind depends on the personal character of the speaker [ethos]; the second on putting the audience into a certain frame of mind [pathos]; the third on the proof, or apparent proof, provided by the words of the speech itself [logos]."

Sir John Whitmore. *Coaching for Performance: Growing Human Potential and Purpose.* Nicholas Brealey Publishing, 1992 (4th ed. 2009).
URL: https://www.performanceconsultants.com/grow-model
Verbatim: "The GROW model is a simple yet powerful framework for structuring a coaching conversation... The skill of the coach lies in knowing which question to ask and when."

Lincoln, Abraham. "Address at Cooper Institute." Delivered February 27, 1860. Published in New York Tribune, February 28, 1860.
URL: https://www.abrahamlincolnonline.org/lincoln/speeches/cooper.htm
Verbatim: "Let us have faith that right makes might, and in that faith, let us, to the end, dare to do our duty as we understand it."

*What is NOT cited and why:* Pop-communication frameworks circulating on social media without academic grounding are excluded. The six models (RIDE, FIRE, GROW, SCRTV, ORER, FFC) are practitioner frameworks; GROW has a specific primary source (Whitmore); RIDE, FIRE, SCRTV, ORER, and FFC are distilled practitioner models whose precise originators are disputed in the training literature and are cited here as functional frameworks rather than attributed to specific authors whose claims cannot be verified.
